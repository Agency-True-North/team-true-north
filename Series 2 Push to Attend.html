01 / What This Series Does

The 3-day countdown timer is running. These eight emails exist to get your lead back to the webclass before it expires.

This series starts the moment the Welcome Email is sent and runs until the end of Day 3. The tone escalates gradually — early emails are curious and warm, later emails are direct and urgent. By Day 3 you are telling them the window is closing and they need to decide.

At a glance Series 2 overview
  • Number of emails: 8
  • Trigger: Immediately following the Welcome Email
  • Duration: Days 0–3 of the urgency window
  • Links to: Urgency Webclass page
  • Goal: Get them to click through and watch before the countdown timer hits zero

04 / Writing Guidance

The examples in Section 03 show you the structure and the arc. Your job is to write your own version of each one — adapted to your ICP, your story, and your voice.

Everything gets adapted

Subject lines, preview text, and body copy all need to reflect your voice and your ICP. Paste each example into Claude and give it context: who you are, who you serve, what pain they are carrying, what tone resonates with them, what they are trying to avoid. Ask Claude to rewrite the email in your voice. Read the result out loud. If it does not sound like something you would actually say to someone you care about, send it back and ask Claude to try again. Do not upload anything that feels off.

The placeholders What goes in each bracket
  • Webclass name. The name of your webclass.
  • Webclass length. How long the webclass is (e.g. 45 minutes, 1 hour). Be accurate.
  • Your pain / your outcome / your timeframe. Your real story. Specific and true. Not inflated.
  • ICP avoid. The things your ideal client is tired of trying or unwilling to do. Write the ones that are true for your audience.
  • ICP pain 1, 2, 3. The daily frustrations your ideal client is living with right now. Specific is better than general.
  • 3rd party name, story, pain, result. A real testimonial from someone on your team or in your network. Use a real person with a real result. Do not make one up.
Itchy bullets How to write bullets that make people click

An itchy bullet is a one-line teaser that creates a need-to-know feeling without actually answering the question. It names that something exists — a method, a mistake, a reframe — but withholds just enough that the only way to scratch the itch is to click and watch. Emails 2, 6, and 7 use them to drive replays.

  • Wrong: "How to build a second income stream from home" — this tells them what it is. They don't need to click to find out.
  • Right: "The one thing most women do in the first 30 days that guarantees they never get momentum" — this withholds just enough that they need to know what that thing is.
  • The test: If the bullet gives away the answer, rewrite it. The reader should finish it and think: what is it? That's the itch.
  • Write 5, use 3. Draft more than you need, then pick the three that create the strongest pull.
The GIF in Email 1

Email 1 calls for a GIF. This is a short animated clip — a few seconds pulled from your webclass that acts as a visual preview. It gives the reader a taste of what's inside without telling them everything. If you don't have one yet, a strong text-only version of this email works fine. Add the GIF when you have it.

The photo in Email 3

Email 3 uses a testimonial with a photo. Use a real person from your team or network — someone whose result is genuine and who has given you permission to share their story. A photo makes the story feel real. No photo is better than a stock photo.

05 / GHL Setup

The workflow already exists. You are adding your email content into the actions that are already set up — not building from scratch.

Write first, upload second

All eight emails should be written, reviewed, and finalized in Google Docs before you touch GHL. Upload only when every email is exactly how you want it.

Step by step Uploading Series 2 in GHL
  • Go to Automations > Workflows in GHL.
  • Open: 2. Pipeline 1 & 2 (The True North Method)
  • Open: THE TRUE NORTH METHOD WEBCLASS - LEAD TO SIGN UP
  • Open: 2- Webclass - Haven't heard back, reminder to Watch
  • Find each email action in the workflow. There are 8 email actions — one for each send time. Upload your emails in order, matching each email to the correct action and send time.
  • For each email action: paste in your subject line, add your preview text, paste in your personalized body copy, and set the link to your Urgency Webclass URL.
  • Send yourself a test for each email. Check that links work and the email reads cleanly on your phone.
  • Save in two places for each action:
    • Click Save Action inside the email action itself.
    • Click Save in the top right corner of the workflow.
Every link in every email goes to the Urgency Webclass

All eight emails in this series link to the Urgency Webclass page — not the Evergreen page, not the checkout page. Double-check every link before saving. One wrong URL in one email breaks the timer experience for that lead.
