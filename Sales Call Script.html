Hell → Heaven Framework

A line-by-line guide for running a sales call that feels like connection and closes with clarity.
Sales Hell → Heaven Discovery Calls
Before you open this script

The True North app has a built-in Hell → Heaven skill that walks you through this in real time. Use it as your primary tool. This guide is your training reference and backup — use it to learn the framework, study the language, and build fluency before you get on calls.

This is not a word-for-word script to read aloud. It is a framework to internalize. The goal is to know it well enough that it sounds completely like you.

Color Key

Say Exact language to use. Make it yours, but stay close.
Coaching Note What new callers most often get wrong here.
Don't Say / Don't Do Common mistakes that cost you the call.
Listen For Signals that tell you what to do next.
Move Forward When The signal to advance to the next phase.
If / Then Branching decisions based on their response.

Call Map — Total: 45–60 Minutes

  1. 01 Frame the Call 3–5 min
  2. 02 Hell: Dive Into the Pain 15–20 min
  3. 03 Heaven: Cast the Vision 10–15 min
  4. 04 Close the Commitment
  5. 05 Finances + Resourcefulness 5–10 min
  6. 06 Spouse Objection Pre-Frame if needed
  7. 07 Separate Money from Commitment if needed
  8. 08 Inoculate Against Cold Feet every call
  9. 09 Affiliating On the Call if closing today

00 / Before the Call

Walk into every call with context. If you're reading notes cold while they're talking, you've already lost the lead.

Review before every call

  • Review their clarity form or clarity call notes — know their name, their situation, and what they responded to
  • Know why they raised their hand: what they clicked on, what they said on the form
  • Have the True North Hell → Heaven skill open in the app as your primary reference
  • Close everything else. Give this call your full attention.
  • Have a notepad ready — you will need their exact words in Phase 4
Why the form matters

If they filled out a clarity form, you already know something about their pain before they say a word. Use it. Reference it early. "I noticed you mentioned X on the form — tell me more about that." It builds immediate rapport and shows you actually read what they shared.

01 / Phase One

Phase 01
Frame the Call
Set the container before the conversation begins. This is not small talk — it is the moment you establish yourself as the guide and create the safety for them to be completely honest with you.
3–5 minutes
◆ Say
"Just want to make sure you have 45–60 minutes to be fully present with me on this call?"
No time? Reschedule. A rushed call does not close — and it shouldn't.
◆ Say
"Good. This might be one of the most important conversations of your life."
Let it land. Don't soften it or follow it with something lighter. Mean it.
◆ Say
"My job here is to connect with you and guide you — not to tell you what you want to hear, but what you need to hear to make the most aligned decision for your dreams. Does that make sense?"
"Does that make sense?" is not filler. It is a micro-close. Use it throughout the call. Every small yes compounds into the big one.
◆ Say
"I'm sure you have questions for me too — there will be plenty of room for that."
✕ Don't Do
  • Don't apologize for taking their time or minimize how important this call is
  • Don't skip the frame because it feels awkward — it is doing real psychological work
  • Don't move on until time is confirmed and the frame is agreed to
→ Move Forward When

They have confirmed their time and agreed to the frame you just set. Both. Not just one.

02 / Phase Two

Phase 02
Hell: Dive Into the Pain
Help them feel and name what their current life is actually costing them. The deeper you go here, the more invested they become in finding a solution. You are not manufacturing urgency — you are helping them see what is already true.
15–20 minutes
Open the Conversation
◆ Say
"So — what's going on that made you want to start an online business right now in your life?"
Listen more than you speak. Take notes. Their exact words will matter in Phase 4.
Extract the Emotional and Relational Cost
◆ Say
"What is that costing you personally?"
Wait for a real answer. Don't move on if they give you a surface response. Push gently: "And what else?"
◆ Say
"How does that affect your kids? Your spouse?"
Get specific. Ask for names. Ask for ages. Ask about their schedule, their job, their daily life. The more specific the details, the more real the conversation becomes. This is care, not nosiness — and they will feel the difference.

Inoculate Against "I Need to Think About It"
✓ Coaching Note

Do this before the objection ever forms. You are planting the answer to "I need to think about it" while you're still in the pain phase. Once they've answered this question, that objection has already been addressed — they said it themselves.

◆ Say
"Why is it important for you to do something about this now — not six months from now?"
Alternate phrasing "What's the tug on your heart that's making you want to move on this now, instead of waiting?"
Let them answer fully. Do not jump in. Silence here is productive.
Pre-Frame Why This Time Is Different
◆ Say
"What else have you tried before?"
◆ Say
"Why didn't that work?"
◆ Say
"What makes you feel like this time would be different?"
◆ Say
"How are you going to show up differently this time?"
This question gets them to commit out loud to a new level of personal responsibility. When they say what they'll do differently, they believe it more than if you said it for them.
✕ Don't Do
  • Do not talk about the business, the product, or the opportunity in this phase — not yet
  • Do not rush through the pain to get to the solution. The pain phase is doing the most important work of the call
  • Do not fill silences. Silence is where the real answers come from
✓ Coaching Note

This is the phase where most callers get impatient and want to start talking about the business. The instinct is to rescue them from their pain by showing them the solution. Don't. Stay in their pain. The more time they spend naming what their life is costing them, the more motivated they are to change it. Your job here is to ask, listen, and ask again.

→ Move Forward When

They have clearly named what their current life is costing them — personally, relationally, emotionally. They have told you what they've tried before and why it didn't work. You can feel the weight of their situation.

03 / Phase Three

Phase 03
Heaven: Cast the Vision
Help them feel the life they actually want — specifically. Not vague hopes. Specific pictures. The goal is to make the vision so clear and so felt that staying stuck starts to feel more painful than taking the leap.
10–15 minutes
Open the Vision
◆ Say
"Dream with me. In 3–5 years — what would your ideal life look like?"
Give them time. If they stay vague, push for specifics.
◆ Say — Prompt for Specifics
"What kind of house? What are you driving? What does your schedule actually look like? Where are you traveling? What's your income — monthly? Yearly?"
Keep going until you have a picture in your head. Vague answers = they haven't fully gone there yet. Push gently until the vision is specific.

Bridge to Decision
◆ Say

Wait for their answer. Don't rush past this. It is the bridge from dreaming to deciding.
◉ Listen For
  • Specific details — house, car, schedule, dollar amounts. Specificity = they're really feeling it
  • Emotion in their voice — a shift in tone, a pause, their voice getting quieter or fuller
  • Family mentions — when they bring up their kids or partner unprompted, the vision is landing
✕ Don't Move On Yet If
  • Their vision is still vague ("I just want more time" / "I want to make more money") — keep pushing for specifics
  • You can't hear the emotion in their voice — they're describing it intellectually, not feeling it. Go deeper.
✓ Coaching Note

This is where the energy of the call shifts. Up until now you've been excavating pain. Now you're building desire. Write down their specific words — the exact vision they describe. You will reflect it back to them word for word in Phase 4. Paraphrase is not the same thing.

→ Move Forward When
  • They have described a specific vision — not general, not vague
  • You can hear or feel the emotion behind it
  • If either is missing — keep pushing. Go back to the specifics questions. They are not ready yet.

04 / Phase Four

Phase 04
Close the Commitment
You've heard their pain. You've helped them build their vision. Now you reflect it back exactly and make a clear, confident recommendation. This is not a soft suggestion.
Recap in Their Exact Words
◆ Say
"Here's what I heard. You're stuck in [their pain], and it's costing you and your family [their impact]. And what you want is [their desired future]."
Use their exact words. Not your summary — theirs. This is not the moment to paraphrase. They need to hear themselves reflected back precisely. That's what makes it land.
✓ Coaching Note

This is why you take notes in Phase 2 and Phase 3. If you can't recall their exact language, you can't do this right. The goal is for them to feel completely heard — more heard than they've felt in a long time.

Make the Recommendation
◆ Say
"My recommendation is to get started with a Quad. That positions you to maximize your commissions right away."
★  Then stop. Completely. Do not add more. Do not explain. Do not soften. Let them speak first. Whoever speaks next is the one who needs something.
✕ Don't Do
  • Do not fill the silence after the recommendation — this is where most people lose the deal
  • Do not follow your recommendation with "Does that sound good?" or any softening phrase
  • Do not start explaining the packages unprompted — wait for them to engage
If / Then
→ If they engage and start discussing options: Move into package details.
→ If they go quiet or show hesitation around financing: Move to Phase 5.

05 / Phase Five

Phase 05
Finances + Resourcefulness
Starting a business requires investment. Your job is not to be their financial advisor — it's to help them think resourcefully about what options actually exist. Successful people aren't always more resourced. They're more resourceful.
5–10 minutes
◆ Say
"Starting a business takes an investment. What resources do you have available?"
Let them answer first, fully. Then prompt if needed.
◆ Say — Prompt if Needed
"Checking or savings? Credit cards? 401k? Home equity? What does your credit look like?"
◆ Say
"You've obviously invested in yourself or your current situation before. What have you used to finance things in the past?"
◆ Say — Reframe
"Successful people aren't always more resourced — they're more resourceful. We're just exploring options together. This doesn't mean you'll use any of them."
✕ Don't Do

Don't let this feel like an interrogation. You're problem-solving alongside them — not extracting financial information. Warm and curious, not transactional.

06 / Phase Six

Phase 06
Spouse Objection Pre-Frame
Find out where the spouse stands before the objection shows up after you hang up. Better to address it now than to lose the deal to a conversation you weren't part of.
If needed
Use if a partner was mentioned but is not on the call
◆ Say
"I heard you mention your husband. Is he supportive of you doing this?"
If / Then
→ If yes: "Good. But he's not on this call — so what happens if he pushes back when you talk to him later? Do we just let that stop you, or do you find a way?"
→ If no or unsure: Explore what his concerns are and what support would need to look like. You may need to arrange a three-way call.
✓ Coaching Note

Don't skip this phase if a partner was mentioned anywhere in the call. A deal that closes on the call can fall apart in 48 hours if the spouse conversation hasn't been addressed. Pre-framing it here gives her language and resolve for what's coming.

07 / Phase Seven

Phase 07
Separate Money from Commitment
Most people who "can't afford it" have not yet fully committed. Commitment comes first. The how comes second. This phase separates those two things.
If needed
Use when money is the stated barrier
◆ Say
"Let's set the money aside completely for a second. Are you committed to this?"
Wait. Don't help them answer. Don't soften the question or give them an out. Their answer is the data.
◆ Say
"Because committed people don't wait until they know the 'how.' They decide first. Then they figure it out."
✓ Coaching Note

This is not a pressure tactic. It is a clarity question. You are helping them separate wanting the result from believing they can get there. Those are two different things, and most people conflate them. A person who is truly committed will find a way. A person who is not will use money as the reason — and no financing conversation will change that.

08 / Phase Eight

Phase 08
Inoculate Against Cold Feet
Protect the decision they just made. Once you hang up, they go back to their world — their friends, their family, their own doubts. This conversation prepares them for that. Use it on every call, regardless of where they land.
Every call
✓ Coaching Note — Read This First

Deliver this conversationally, in your own words. This is the one phase you should not read from the script. Know the structure, know the points, and let it come out naturally. A scripted inoculation defeats its own purpose.

Cover These Points — In Your Own Words
◉ Hit Each of These
  • Big decisions feel clear in the moment — then doubt can creep in after
  • When you hang up, you'll talk to people you love. Some will question it. Some might call it a scam. That is not sabotage — that is fear-based protection. But they weren't on this call. They don't have the context you have right now.
  • You might also get in your own head. Second-guess yourself. Wonder if you have what it takes. That is where most people stop. That is the line between people who build something and people who stay exactly where they are.
◆ Close With This Question
"If your family pushes back — if you get cold feet — if financing takes longer than expected — what happens? Do we toss everything we talked about out the window? Do we walk away from the future you just described to me? Or do you double down on your commitment? Talk to me. What happens next?"
This is not rhetorical. Wait for a real answer. Their response tells you exactly how real this deal is and what your follow-up conversation needs to look like.
◉ Listen For
  • Committed person: Answers with something concrete. "I'm doing this." "I'll figure it out." "My husband will come around." Clear. Decisive.
  • Lukewarm person: Goes vague. Starts hedging. Makes it conditional. "Well, I guess it depends…" This tells you the follow-up needs more work — and it's better to know now.

09 / Phase Nine

Phase 09
Affiliating On the Call
If they are ready to move forward today, this is where it becomes official. How you handle this moment is the first impression of what it's like to work with you. Be organized. Be calm. Be thorough.
If closing today
  • 1 Share your screen. Let them watch you fill out the forms in real time — they should be able to follow every step.
  • 2 Complete both forms together. Walk them through each field. Don't rush.
  • 3 GHL records automatically — you cannot stop it. After the call, go into the transcript and delete the section where card details were collected. Do not leave payment information in any transcript.
  • 4 Refer to the SOP for direct form links and the full affiliating process.
  • 5 Send all completed forms to Naomi immediately after the call, with the exact package confirmed.
✕ Don't Do

Do not rush through the forms. Moving fast creates mistakes and erodes their confidence right at the moment they most need to feel they made the right call. They are watching how you work. Organized and calm is the only way to close this.
