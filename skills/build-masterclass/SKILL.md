# SKILL: build-masterclass

## The Governing Law — Sell the Emotion, Not the Thing

Nike doesn't sell shoes. They sell empowerment.
Disney doesn't sell a theme park. They sell magic and memories with your family.
Volvo doesn't sell cars. They sell the feeling of safety.

The masterclass never sells the product, the system, or the information. It sells the EMOTION underneath the audience's pains, desires, and fears — the relief, the freedom, the pride, the safety, the belonging they're actually chasing.

Every instruction below serves this law. Before a single word of script is written, name the emotional core in one sentence:

> **"We are not selling [the thing]. We are selling [the deep emotion]."**

That sentence is the spine. Every beat of the script hangs on it. If a line doesn't connect to that feeling, cut it or rewrite it until it does.

---

## When to Use / What It Produces

Run this skill when you have a completed ICP (from the upstream research skill) and need a finished masterclass. It does not do audience research — it receives the ICP and turns it into a deeply personal, emotionally driven presentation. It produces two things, in order: **Phase 1**, the full spoken script start to finish (the emotional draft, complete on its own), then **Phase 2**, a numbered slide-by-slide breakdown separating the on-screen line from the spoken presenter notes. On request it adds **Phase 3**: building the actual Canva deck from the Phase 2 map (see below). A new team member with zero copywriting skill should get both in one sitting.

If the business has a brand voice guide or a pre-publish quality gate, load the voice guide before writing and run the gate on the finished output.

## Inputs

### 1. The ICP contract (primary input)

The upstream skill is `/build-icp` — its dossier ends with a HANDOFF BLOCK that matches this contract field for field. A hand-written ICP in the same shape works too.

The skill expects these fields. Where one is thin or missing, ask ONE plain targeted question to fill it, then proceed. Don't stall the build on a full re-interview. If there's no ICP at all, stop and run `/build-icp` first — never invent one.

- **IDENTITY** — who they are: role, life stage, what the "success" version of them looks like from the outside.
- **THEIR TUESDAY** — a real, ordinary day in their life, beat by beat.
- **SURFACE PAINS → EMOTION BENEATH** — each pain plus the feeling under it (e.g. "no time" → the grief of missing their own life).
- **DESIRES → EMOTION BENEATH** — each want plus the feeling it promises (e.g. "more income" → the safety of never bracing at the grocery checkout).
- **FEARS → EMOTION BENEATH** — what they're afraid of and the feeling under it (e.g. "what if it doesn't work" → the shame of hoping out loud again).
- **FAILED FIXES** — what they already tried that didn't hold.
- **THEIR LANGUAGE** — the actual words and phrases they use about their life. Quote these verbatim in the script.
- **THE REAL TRANSFORMATION** — the emotional end-state they're chasing, stated as a feeling, not a metric.

### 2. Presenter intake (short — fewest questions possible)

The ICP covers the audience. This covers the speaker. Collect:

1. **Before-story and turning point** — who they were before, and the moment it changed (fuels Reverse Authority and Reluctant Hero).
2. **Proof** — results, story, or evidence they can honestly stand on.
3. **The ONE big promise** of the class.
4. **The 3 discoveries** they'll teach (or the raw material to shape them).
5. **The offer + CTA** — name, what's in it, price, terms, bonuses, checkout link. **Confirm against the CURRENT live offer before presenting. Never hardcode a price or terms from memory.**

## Step 1 — Name the Emotional Core

Read the ICP's pains, desires, and fears columns. Ignore the surface line. Look at the EMOTION BENEATH column and find the one feeling that repeats — the thing every pain, want, and fear points at.

Write the sentence:

> We are not selling **[the product/system/class]**. We are selling **[that one feeling]**.

Examples of the move:
- ICP of exhausted high-earners → "We are not selling a business system. We are selling the feeling of getting your own life back."
- ICP of new empty-nesters → "We are not selling a fitness program. We are selling proof that she still gets a turn."

Put this sentence at the top of the working doc. Check every section against it as you write.

---

## Step 2 — The Build Guide (17 parts — never reorder)

The class renders as a slide deck. Each slide carries ONE short beat — a single punchy line, sometimes three words. The full spoken script lives in presenter notes. A real masterclass runs 300–440 slides because each sentence often gets its own slide. One idea, one slide. Never cram.

Write Phase 1 as continuous script through these 17 parts, in this order.

### OPEN

**1. TITLE** — *Job: intrigue, don't explain.*
Template: 2–5 punchy words. Not a description.
Example: *The Space You Lost*

**2. SUB-HEADLINE** — *Job: the big benefit of attending.*
Template: "How to [benefit] without [pain]."
Example: *How to build income that runs without you — without adding one hour to your week.*

**3. HOOK / ANCHOR** — *Job: earn them staying to the end with an open loop.*
Template: promise a bonus, surprise, or unexplained benefit paid off only at the end.
Example: *Stay to the end. There's something waiting there you won't find anywhere else — and it's not what you think.*

**4. DIFFERENTIATOR** — *Job: answer "how is this different from everything I've tried?"*
Template: name what they're used to (pull from FAILED FIXES), then say why this isn't that.
Example: *You've bought the course. Joined the program. Watched it gather dust by Thursday. This isn't more information to keep up with. It's less.*

**5. DISSECT** — *Job: attract your person, repel the wrong one. Good marketing repels on purpose.*
Template: "This is for you if [ideal traits]. This is not for you if [worst-customer traits + the pain they'll keep]."
Example: *If you're tired and still willing — stay. If you want someone to blame, this isn't your room.*

**6. NO SURPRISES** — *Job: disarm the "you're just going to sell me" wall.*
Template (near-verbatim, it works): *"At the end, there will be something you can buy — if you love what you learn and want results faster. No obligation. Deal?"*

**7. REVERSE AUTHORITY** — *Job: get off the pedestal. Relatable beats impressive.*
Template: "I wasn't always ___, ___, or ___. Not long ago I was ___." Use the presenter's real before-story.
Example: *I wasn't always the woman with the calm mornings. Three years ago I was reheating the same coffee at 4 p.m., wondering when my life had become a to-do list.*

**8. FUTURE PACE** — *Job: put them inside the finished life.*
Template: "What would it be like if…" — paint the ICP's REAL TRANSFORMATION in sensory detail, then close the loop: "That's what's possible if you stay to the end."
Example: *What would it be like if the alarm went off… and nothing in you braced? That's what's possible if you stay with me to the end.*

**9. ATTENTION** — *Job: ask for full presence.*
Template: "Let's make a deal…" + specific asks.
Example: *Let's make a deal. Phone face-down, kids in the other room, tabs closed. Give me thirty minutes that are actually yours.*

### THE PROVEN OPEN (default — run it across parts 7–8)

Before the presenter introduces themselves, mirror the audience's real day back to them — pulled straight from THEIR TUESDAY — one ordinary beat at a time, until they feel SEEN. Then lift the shame off them. The shape:

1. *"Before I introduce myself, I want to take a guess at what I think your Tuesday looks like."*
2. Walk their Tuesday beat by beat, concrete and sensory — the alarm and the body already tired, the coffee gone cold and reheated and forgotten again, the whole day performed without dropping a thing.
3. Land the quiet line they say to themselves — pull it verbatim from THEIR LANGUAGE. *"And somewhere in the quiet you think: someday. Someday it won't feel like this. But tomorrow looked exactly the same."*
4. Lift the shame: *"And you started to wonder if the problem was you. Let me be clear — it was never you. You didn't lose ambition. You lost space."*
5. Bridge to Reverse Authority: *"And if you're anything like me, you already tried to fix it."*

That's Reverse Authority + Future Pace fused with their real pain and the emotion beneath it. The whole open earns the right to teach. Reproduce this move from any ICP — the beats change, the shape doesn't.

### BODY

**10. THE 3 DISCOVERIES** — *Job: name the three things they're about to learn — and sink the hook deeper.*
Template: frame each as a belief-breaker — name the false belief keeping them stuck, promise the discovery that breaks it. Benefit-driven headlines.
Example: *Discovery one — why working harder is the exact thing keeping you stuck. And what actually creates room to breathe.*

**11. BODY** — *Job: deliver the three. Keep it simple. Confused people don't buy.*
Template per discovery: false belief → the break (proof or story) → the benefit stated as a FEELING. Every point showcases the benefit and the feeling, not just the information. Do not overwhelm — they don't need everything you know.
Example beat: *Picture checking your phone on a Tuesday morning and seeing the system worked while you slept. That feeling is the point — not the mechanics.*

**12. RECAP** — *Job: tell them what you told them, phrased as benefits.*
Template: three lines, one per discovery — "How to [X] without [Y]."
Example: *How to build income that compounds — without giving it your evenings.*

### CLOSE

**13. SEGWAY** — *Job: bridge from teaching to offer.*
Template: *"This has been powerful, right? But here's the truth — information alone won't save you. What if you could [main benefit]?"*

**14. REVEAL** — *Job: reveal the offer as the answer to the feeling.*
Template: *"That's why we created [offer] — to help people like you [benefit] without [pain]."*

**15. VALUE STACK / MICRO-CLOSE** — *Job: break the offer into parts, value each, start closing BEFORE the price.*
Template: Product · Price · Terms · Bonuses. Name each component, what it does for them (as a feeling), and its "valued at." Weave 1–2 of the selected closes between components.

**16. JUSTIFY / PRICE DROP** — *Job: make the price feel small next to the feeling.*
Template: *"If all it did was [one emotional result] — wouldn't it be worth it?"* Justify the full "valued at," then drop: *"I wanted this in reach of anyone who's serious… so it's not [high anchor]. It's [real price]."*

**17. CHECKOUT / FUTURE PACE** — *Job: remove every ounce of friction.*
Template: walk the exact clicks. *"When you click the button, you'll land on a page that looks like this. Fill in the usual details. And here's what happens the moment you say yes…"* Then future-pace once more — them, on the other side, feeling the emotional core.

## Step 3 — Close Selection (pick 4–6 of the 17)

Never run all 17 closes. Read the ICP's FEARS → EMOTION BENEATH and pick 4–6 that answer those specific fears. Weave them through parts 13–17.

Match fears to closes:

| The ICP's fear underneath | Closes that answer it |
|---|---|
| Money guilt — "spending on myself is selfish/risky" | 1. Money Is Good · 2. Disposable Income · 3. Money Replenishes |
| "I've been burned — another thing that won't deliver" (heavy FAILED FIXES) | 4. Break Old Habits · 5. Information Alone · 13. If You Only Get |
| Shame of hoping out loud again / "the problem is me" | 7. Responsibility · 14. Reluctant Hero · 16. Parts Pattern |
| Fear of staying stuck — the years slipping by | 12. Now / Later · 9. Your 2 Choices · 10. Say Goodbye |
| Identity — who they want to be vs. who they've been | 11. Us vs. Them · 15. Portal Close |
| Price resistance specifically | 8. My 2 Choices · 13. If You Only Get |

Full reference list: 1 Money Is Good · 2 Disposable Income · 3 Money Replenishes · 4 Break Old Habits · 5 Information Alone · 6 Result or Excuses · 7 Responsibility · 8 My 2 Choices · 9 Your 2 Choices · 10 Say Goodbye · 11 Us vs. Them · 12 Now / Later · 13 If You Only Get · 14 Reluctant Hero · 15 Portal Close · 16 Parts Pattern · 17 Close Close.

**Order to run them:** dissolve the belief first (money/failed-fix closes, inside the value stack) → cost of inaction (Now/Later, Your 2 Choices, at the price drop) → identity (Us vs. Them, Portal, Parts Pattern, after the price) → **always end on 17. Close Close** — the final, direct call to action.

Note on tone: closes like Result or Excuses (6) and Us vs. Them (11) run hot. Soften them to the audience — invitation, not accusation. Never guilt, never shame.

## Step 4 — The Two-Phase Output (always in this order, both shown)

### PHASE 1 — THE SCRIPT

First, print the emotional core sentence: *"We are not selling ___. We are selling ___."*

Then write the FULL spoken masterclass, start to finish — every word the presenter says — as continuous readable script following the 17-part spine, driven entirely by the ICP's emotions. It reads like a story, not a slide list. It is the emotional draft, complete on its own. Mark the 17 section boundaries with small headers so the presenter can navigate, but the prose flows straight through.

### PHASE 2 — THE SLIDE BREAKDOWN

Take the finished Phase 1 script and break it into numbered slide beats. Every beat in this exact two-part format:

```
── [section name, e.g. OPEN › Future Pace] ──
SLIDE:  <the one short line on screen — often 3–7 words>
SCRIPT: <the spoken words for that beat, from Phase 1, that live
        in the presenter notes>
```

Rules for the split:
- One idea per slide. If a slide line carries two ideas, it's two slides.
- The SLIDE line is the emotional punch or anchor — the noun-and-adjective heart of the beat, not a summary.
- The SCRIPT is everything spoken around that line, lifted from Phase 1. Nothing spoken gets lost in the split.
- Keep beats in script order. Number them straight through (1, 2, 3…).
- Expect volume: a full class lands around 300–440 beats. Short is a red flag that ideas got crammed.

The result is a deck-ready map a person can build slide by slide.

### PHASE 3 — THE CANVA DECK (optional, on request)

The Canva connection can turn Phase 2 into an actual Canva presentation — SLIDE lines on the slides, SCRIPT in the presenter notes. One route does this faithfully:

**The HTML import route (use this one).**
1. Generate an HTML file from Phase 2: one element per beat with `data-document-role="page"`, the SLIDE line as the page content (big, centered, one line), `data-label` = the beat's section name, and `data-speaker-notes` = the full SCRIPT text.
2. The file must be reachable at a public HTTPS URL for Canva to ingest it — `import-design-from-url` cannot read local files, and private content must never be pushed to a file-sharing service to fake one. **Stop and get the owner's explicit OK first** — the full script is publicly reachable at that URL during the import, and public URLs can be cached even after deletion. Take the page down right after import.
3. Import with `intended_design_type: presentation`. Verify with `get-design-pages` (page count matches beat count) and `get-presenter-notes` (spot-check the notes landed).
4. The import gives structure, not styling. Final look — fonts, backgrounds, brand — gets applied in Canva by a human, or by copying styles from an existing deck.

**What the other routes can't do (don't fight them):**
- `generate-design-structured` AI-rewrites your outline into its own copy. It will not hold the exact slide lines or write presenter notes. Wrong tool for a script where every word was chosen.
- `perform-editing-operations` can find-and-replace text in an EXISTING deck (useful for updating a copy of a current masterclass deck when the slide count matches) but cannot add pages or write presenter notes. Rebuild-by-edit only works slide-for-slide.

If Phase 3 is skipped or declined, the Phase 2 map is the deliverable — a human builds the deck from it slide by slide.

## Voice Rules (non-negotiable)

- **Second person.** Talk to one person, not a room.
- **Short sentences. One idea per line.** Rhythm over polish.
- **Grounded, specific, human.** No hype, no guru-speak, no woo, no jargon.
- **Lead from certainty and the positive.** Paint the desired life. Do NOT name the doubt — never say scam, pyramid, or MLM. Naming it plants it.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "Coffee went cold, you reheated it" beats "you felt overwhelmed." Then name the FEELING, not just the fact.
- **Use their language.** Quote THEIR LANGUAGE fields verbatim at least three times across the script.
- If the business has a banned-word or brand-voice guide, it applies to every line.

## Compliance (hard rules)

- **No income guarantees. No "typical earnings."** If income comes up, frame it as possibility with a clear results-vary disclaimer ON THE SLIDE where it appears.
- Make the aspirational case honestly — proof and story, not hype.
- **The offer/CTA is a fill-in field.** Confirm it against the CURRENT company offer — product, price, terms, bonuses — before presenting. Never hardcode.

---

## Quality Checklist (pass/fail — every item must pass)

1. Is the emotional core named in one sentence before a word of script was written?
2. Does every beat connect to a feeling, not just a fact?
3. Does the open mirror their real Tuesday — beat by beat, from the ICP — before the speaker introduces themselves?
4. Does the open lift the shame off them ("it was never you")?
5. Are all 17 parts present, in order, nothing reordered or skipped?
6. Is it second person throughout — one person, not a room?
7. One idea per slide, SLIDE lines short and punchy (mostly 3–7 words)?
8. Is the ICP's verbatim language quoted at least three times?
9. Are each of the 3 discoveries framed as belief-breakers with benefit-driven headlines?
10. Are 4–6 closes selected to match THIS audience's specific fears, ending on the Close Close?
11. Is income framed as possibility with a results-vary disclaimer — never guaranteed, never "typical"?
12. Does it lead from certainty and never name the doubt — and is the offer confirmed against the current live offer?

If any item fails, fix it before showing the draft. Then run any pre-publish quality gate the business uses, and present with trade-offs — the owner is the source, the final call is theirs.

## Guardrails

- **Sell the emotion, not the thing.** The governing law wins every conflict.
- **The 17-part spine is fixed.** Fix the words inside it, never the structure.
- **One idea, one slide.** Never cram.
- **Phase 1 before Phase 2, always both.** The script is the source of truth — the deck map is derived from it.
- **Voice rules and compliance rules are hard rules,** not suggestions.
- **Confirm the live offer before presenting.** Every time.
- **Two different ICPs through this skill should produce two classes with the same spine and voice but completely different emotional cores** — each feeling like it was written for that one person alone. If the two drafts could swap audiences, the ICP wasn't used hard enough. Go back to Step 1.
