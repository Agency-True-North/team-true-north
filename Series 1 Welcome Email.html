01 / What This Series Does

Someone just opted in. They gave you their email. This is the first thing they hear from you. Make it count.

The Welcome Email does three things: confirms they made the right call signing up, delivers the link to watch, and opens a two-way conversation by asking them to reply. That last part matters more than it looks — replies train the inbox to treat your emails as wanted, which protects your deliverability for everything that follows.

At a glance Series 1 overview
  • Number of emails: 1
  • Trigger: Contact submits opt-in form
  • Send time: Immediately
  • Links to: Urgency Webclass page
  • Goal: Get them to click and watch before the 3-day timer runs out

02 / The Email

The subject line is tested and proven. Do not change it. Everything else — the body copy, the tone, the language — needs to sound like you.

Subject line is locked

"Congrats, You're In! (Open up)" has been tested and performs. Do not rewrite it, shorten it, or make it "your own." Use it exactly as shown. Add your preview text in GHL when you upload — keep it short and curiosity-driven.

Rewrite the body in your voice

The template below was written in a specific style that may not match yours or your audience's language. Before you fill in the brackets, copy the full template into Claude or ChatGPT and ask it to rewrite the body copy in your voice for your ICP. Give it context: who you are, who you serve, what language resonates with them, and what they're trying to avoid. The structure stays the same. The words should sound like you said them.

The subject line is the only thing that does not get rewritten.

03 / How to Personalize It

Every bracket is a place to tell your story. Once you've rewritten the body in your voice, fill each placeholder with something true and specific to you.

The placeholders What goes in each bracket
  • Your pain. The specific feeling or situation your ideal client is living right now — and that you were living too before this. Be concrete. Not "stressed" but "running on empty and resenting the life I'd worked so hard to build."
  • Your outcome. What changed for you. Specific and true. Not a promise of what they'll get — what actually happened for you.
  • Your timeframe. Honest and real. Do not inflate it.
  • ICP avoid 1, 2, 3. The three things your ideal client is tired of, burned by, or unwilling to try. Common examples: cold outreach, quitting their job, pitching friends and family. Write the ones that are true for your audience.
  • Your name. Sign off as yourself.
Why the P.S. matters

The reply prompt at the end of this email is not filler. When a lead replies, their email provider learns that your emails are wanted. That one reply improves your deliverability for every email in every series that follows. It also tells you exactly what your audience is feeling — use those answers to inform your content and your calls.

Both links go to the same place

"Watch it here" and the P.S.S. at the bottom both link to your Urgency Webclass page. That is intentional — two entry points to the same destination. Make sure both links are correct before the workflow goes live.

04 / GHL Setup

One email, no delay. This is the simplest workflow in your funnel to build.

Write first, upload second

Write and finalize your email in Google Docs before you touch GHL. Do not draft inside GHL. Once it reads exactly the way you want it, then open the workflow and paste it in.

Step by step Uploading Series 1 in GHL

The workflow already exists. You are not building from scratch — you are adding your email content into the action that is already set up.

  • Go to Automations > Workflows in GHL.
  • Open: 2. Pipeline 1 & 2 (The True North Method)
  • Open: THE TRUE NORTH METHOD WEBCLASS - LEAD TO SIGN UP
  • Open: 1 - Webclass Form Submitted
  • Find the action: 1. FORMS LEAD (ALL) - FIRST MESSAGE. This is where you upload your email.
  • Paste in your subject line. Congrats, You're In! (Open up) — do not change it.
  • Add your preview text. Keep it short and curiosity-driven.
  • Paste in your personalized body copy. All placeholders filled, rewritten in your voice, links set to your Urgency Webclass URL.
  • Send yourself a test. Open it on your phone. Confirm both links work and the email looks clean.
  • Save in two places:
    • Click Save Action inside the email action itself.
    • Click Save in the top right corner of the workflow.
Important

Do not send people through this series until Series 2 (Push to Attend) is also built and ready. A lead who gets the Welcome Email and then hears nothing for days has already gone cold. Build the sequences in order before running any leads through them.
