01 / What This Series Does

Series 4 spent seven days building trust with no ask at all. Series 5 is the reopen — the webclass comes back online, and these six emails give leads one more clear invitation to watch it.

This series triggers at the end of the Building Trust sequence. The leads here registered, received the webclass invitation, didn't sign up for the Discovery Process, and then spent the last week just receiving value from you with no pressure and no links. They're not cold. They're not converted.

This series gives them another entry point — not a second-chance checkout push, but a genuine reopen of the webclass itself. They get to watch it as if for the first time, with fresh context and a little more trust in you than they had before.

The urgency is honest: the webclass is open, but you genuinely don't know how long you're leaving it up. That plain uncertainty — said directly — lands more effectively than a manufactured countdown. Let it be real.

At a glance Series 5 overview
  • Number of emails: 6
  • Trigger: End of Series 4 (Building Trust)
  • Duration: 3 days, 2 emails per day
  • Links to: Evergreen Webclass
  • Advance Window: OFF in GHL
  • Goal: Bring them back into the webclass as a second entry point into the funnel

04 / Writing Guidance

This series does something different than Series 2 — it doesn't push, it reopens. The tone is calm, honest, and personal. The invitation is real. So is the uncertainty about how long the door stays open.

Adapt to your voice, not just your name

The minimum adaptation is replacing the sign-off with your name. But don't stop there. Read each email out loud and ask: would I actually say this to someone I care about? If something sounds off — too formal, too casual, not your words — that's the part to rewrite. Paste it into Claude with context about your ICP and ask it to adjust the tone while keeping the core message intact.

The arc What each day is doing
  • Day 1 (Emails 1–2): Open the door and explain what's inside. Email 1 is short — the announcement, nothing more. Email 2 walks them through the masterclass content so they know what they're actually signing up to watch.
  • Day 2 (Emails 3–4): Handle the hesitation. Email 3 reframes the decision as low-stakes — it's forty minutes of information, not a commitment. Email 4 names the emotional block directly: skepticism is protecting them, but cynicism will keep them stuck.
  • Day 3 (Emails 5–6): Final call. Email 5 names what staying put actually is — a choice, not a neutral default. Email 6 is the gentlest close: I can't want this more than you do, but I do want it. The door is open. That's it.
What to keep, what to adapt Working with these templates
  • Keep: The honest uncertainty ("I don't know how long I'm leaving it up"). Don't replace it with a fake countdown. The structure of each email. The low-pressure framing — especially in Emails 3 and 6.
  • Adapt: Your sign-off name. Any "I" statements that reference your personal story — make sure they're true for you. Email 2's bullet points if your masterclass content covers different ground than Shannon's.
  • Do not: Add more urgency than is honest. Add more links. Push harder on Day 3 than you did on Day 1. This series works because it doesn't escalate. Trust the arc.
The webclass link

All six emails link to your Evergreen Webclass — not the Discovery Process / checkout page. Get your Evergreen Webclass URL before you start building in GHL and keep it somewhere accessible. Double-check every link before saving.

05 / GHL Setup

The workflow already exists. You are uploading your email content into the actions that are already set up — not building from scratch.

Write first, upload second

All six emails should be written, reviewed, and finalized in Google Docs before you touch GHL. Upload only when every email is exactly how you want it.

Step by step Uploading Series 5 in GHL
  • Go to Automations > Workflows in GHL.
  • Open: 2. Pipeline 1 & 2 (The True North Method)
  • Open: THE TRUE NORTH METHOD WEBCLASS - LEAD TO SIGN UP
  • Open: 5- Webclass Cart Re-open
  • Find each email action in the workflow. There are 6 email actions — one for each send time. Upload your emails in order, matching each email to the correct action and send time.
  • For each email action: paste in your subject line, add your preview text, paste in your personalized body copy, and set the link to your Evergreen Webclass URL.
  • Send yourself a test for each email. Check that every link opens the webclass and the email reads cleanly on your phone.
  • Save in two places for each action:
    • Click Save Action inside the email action itself.
    • Click Save in the top right corner of the workflow.
Advance Window must be OFF

In the workflow settings for this series, make sure the Advance Window is turned OFF. This ensures emails go out at the exact scheduled times rather than within a delivery window. Leaving it on can cause emails to send outside the intended schedule and break the timing arc across the three days.

Every link in every email goes to the Evergreen Webclass

All six emails in this series link to the Evergreen Webclass — not the Discovery Process / checkout page. This is a second invitation to watch the masterclass, not a second checkout push. Make sure every link points to the right place before saving.
