01 / Meta Ads

The ad is the front door. Everything starts here. When someone clicks, they land on your Registration Page.

Meta ads run across both Instagram and Facebook through a single platform — the Meta Ads Manager. You manage one campaign that covers both networks.

Setup How we run ads on Team True North
  • Placements. We use Meta's Advantage+ placement optimization. Meta decides where your ad shows based on who is most likely to convert. You don't manually select placements.
  • Ad format. Video ads in square format: 1:1 ratio, 1080 x 1080 pixels. This format performs across both feed and Stories without cropping.
  • Where ads link. All ads link directly to the Registration Page. Not the webclass. Not the order page. The Registration Page is always the first stop.

02 / Registration Page

This is the first page in your funnel. Every ad points here. The visitor's one job is to enter their email and opt in.

The messaging on this page offers the free masterclass in exchange for their email. "Get access to the free training." That's the exchange. They give you their email. They get the link to watch.

What to capture Email only. Nothing else.
  • Email address. The only field on the opt-in form.
  • No name, no phone number, no additional fields. Every extra field you add drops your conversion rate. Keep the friction as low as possible. Everything else can be collected later.

After they opt in, they are automatically redirected to the Evergreen Webclass Page where they can start watching immediately.

03 / Evergreen Webclass Page

Where your lead lands the moment they opt in. Simple. Clean. One video. No countdown timer.

This is a VSL-style page — video sales letter. The entire purpose is to get the visitor to start watching. There is no navigation, no distractions, and no offer above the fold. The video does the work.

Page elements What's on the Evergreen page
  • Video. The masterclass. This is the main event.
  • Hidden Delay Action Timer. The buy button is hidden when the page loads and only appears after a set delay. This keeps the offer out of sight until the viewer has had time to engage with the content. It reduces the immediate "this is a sales pitch" reaction.
  • Buy Button. Appears after the delay. Links to the Order / Checkout Page.
This page is used in two places

The Evergreen Webclass Page appears at two points in your funnel. First, immediately after opt-in from the ad. Second, in the Cart Reopen series (Series 5) — when the cart reopens after Re-Engagement, this is the page your emails link back to. No timer. No expiration. Always available.

This is intentional. Meta does not allow countdown timers on ad landing pages. The Evergreen page is always the one your leads land on cold.

04 / Urgency Webclass Page

The same video. More urgency. This is the page your Push to Attend emails link to — not the initial landing page.

The Urgency Webclass is a direct replica of the Evergreen page with one significant addition: a countdown timer. The timer is what creates the 3-day urgency window. When it hits zero, access closes and the page redirects to the Closed Page.

What's different Urgency vs. Evergreen
  • "Limited Time Only" headline. The framing shifts from open access to time-limited access.
  • Countdown timer. A visible timer showing how much time is left before the cart closes.
  • Browser + WiFi cookies. The timer cookies the visitor's browser and network connection. If they close the tab and come back three hours later, the timer picks up where it left off. It is not perfect — a different device or incognito tab will reset it. But it is free and functional for where we are in the build phase.
  • Redirects to the Closed Page when it expires. Once the countdown reaches zero, the page automatically redirects. To set this up, paste your Closed Page URL into the redirect URL field on the Urgency Webclass page in GHL.
GHL — redirect URL and auto expire settings
Important

The Urgency Webclass URL and the Evergreen Webclass URL are different links. Do not mix them up. The Push to Attend emails (Series 2) must link to the Urgency page. The Cart Reopen emails (Series 5) must link to the Evergreen page. Using the wrong link in the wrong series breaks the experience.

05 / Closed Page

The page a visitor lands on when the countdown timer expires. The cart is closed. The presentation is no longer available.

When the Urgency Webclass timer hits zero, it redirects here. The messaging is simple: the window has passed. This page exists to close the loop cleanly so the lead understands the situation, rather than landing on a broken or confusing page.

Keep it simple

The Closed Page does not need to be elaborate. A headline, one or two lines of copy, and that is it. No navigation, no offers, no links. The only job of this page is to communicate clearly that the window has closed. Shannon's example below is the standard to follow.

Example — Masterclass Closed Page
GHL Setup How to configure the redirect on the Urgency page

The redirect to the Closed Page is configured on the Urgency Webclass page inside GHL. Here is what to do:

  • Insert your Closed Page URL into the redirect URL field. This is the link GHL will send the visitor to when the timer expires. It must be your Closed Page — not the Evergreen page, not the checkout page.
  • Set revisit to Auto Expire after 5 days. This controls what happens if someone returns to the Urgency page after the timer has already run out. Auto Expire ensures the page treats them as expired — it will not reset or restart the timer.

Both settings live on the Urgency Webclass page in GHL. Once configured, the timer will redirect to your Closed Page automatically when it hits zero.

What happens next

Leads who reach the Closed Page are automatically moved into the Building Trust / Re-Engagement series (Series 4) in GHL. They will receive 14 emails over 7 days with no selling, no links, and no masterclass mentions. The sole purpose of that series is to keep the relationship warm. After it ends, the Cart Reopen series (Series 5) runs and access reopens via the Evergreen Webclass page.

06 / Order / Checkout Page

Where leads go to purchase. Linked from the buy button on both webclass pages, and linked directly from the Push to Cart Close emails on Day 4.

This is a one-step order form. No two-step checkout. The visitor fills out their payment details and submits. That's it.

Page content What the checkout page should include
  • Package name and description. What exactly they are getting access to.
  • Bonuses and total value breakdown. List any included bonuses with their individual values. Show the total value versus what they actually pay.
  • One-step form only. No multi-step or two-page checkout. Single form. Single submit.
  • Money-back guarantee language. The Discovery Process is backed by a money-back guarantee. This should be stated clearly on the page.
Day 4 — Push to Cart Close

The three Day 4 emails in Series 3 skip the webclass entirely and link directly to this page. By Day 4, the urgency window has closed. These emails are not asking leads to watch — they are asking them to decide. The checkout page is the destination.

07 / The Discovery Process

The $99 entry point. Not a commitment. Not a coaching call. A structured evaluation that gives your lead a clear answer in either direction.

The Discovery Process is what the checkout page sells. Here is how to frame it when you write about it — to your leads and to yourself.

How to frame it What the Discovery Process actually is
  • It is not a commitment to a business. It is not quitting your job. It is not signing on to anything long-term. It is a structured space to find out whether this path actually fits your life.
  • It is backed by a money-back guarantee. This is not a risk. If a lead goes through it and this is not the right fit, they get their $99 back and they walk away with real clarity. That is still a win.
  • There are two outcomes. Both are honest. Both are good.
    Not a fit: They get their $99 back and they know something they didn't know before. That is worth $99 on its own.
    Is a fit: They step into a business, a community, and a path that could change their family's life.
  • You don't know what you don't know. If someone is sitting on the fence, something made them sign up. That is worth sitting with. The Discovery Process is how they find out what that something means.
A note on language

Never call the Discovery Process a "coaching call" or a "strategy session." Those words carry a different expectation. This is not advice-giving. It is evaluation. It is clarity. The framing matters because the lead's expectations going in will shape whether they feel the outcome was worth it.

Never use income claims, specific earnings, or financial promises anywhere near this page or the emails that lead to it.
