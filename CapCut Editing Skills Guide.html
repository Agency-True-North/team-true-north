01 / What You're Installing

Two editing helpers that live inside Claude Code. You drag in a video, Claude drags out a finished draft.

/rough-cut Short talking-head videos

Full treatment: word-level captions, a typed-in hook, timed on-screen annotations with sounds, optional hot-take labels, pop words, and b-roll pulled from your own library. You approve every text layer in chat before anything gets built.

/long-cut Long 16:9 videos (masterclass style)

Cuts filler words, dead pauses, flubs, and retakes. Plain draft, no captions or overlays — you add those yourself. Built for cleaning up long-form footage fast.

You never touch code

You never open Terminal and you never edit a settings file. Claude runs every command. Your part is clicking around CapCut and answering a couple of plain-language questions the first time you set it up.

02 / Before You Start

  • A Mac. This kit is Mac only.
  • The CapCut desktop app, installed from capcut.com. Open it once after installing so it creates its own folders.
  • Claude Code, on a paid Claude plan. Section 03 below covers this if you don't have it yet.
  • About 30 minutes for the one-time setup. You only do it once.
  • A free Deepgram account for transcription — Claude walks you through creating it during setup. A typical video costs about a cent to transcribe.

03 / Get Claude Code (If You Don't Have It)

Get Set Up claude.com/code

Go to claude.com/code and sign in with a paid Claude plan. Use the desktop app version — it's the non-technical-friendly option for this kit. No Terminal and no code editor required; the app gives you a chat window where you open a folder and talk to Claude directly.

Already using Claude Code for something else?

Same login. This kit lives in its own folder and doesn't depend on any other Claude setup you've built — it works standalone.

04 / Download The Kit

⇩  Download the Kit (.zip)

Contains rough-cut + long-cut + the setup script Claude runs

Step 1 Unzip it

Double-click the downloaded file. You get a folder called capcut-kit.

Step 2 Drag the folder into your Claude folder

Move the whole capcut-kit folder into your Claude folder — the same one that holds your ABOUT ME and PROJECTS folders — and keep the name exactly as it is. That's the entire manual part of this install.

Don't have a Claude folder yet?

Create one anywhere you'll remember on your computer, called exactly Claude, and put capcut-kit inside it. It doesn't matter whether it's on your Desktop or in Documents — what matters is that it's a folder you'll open Claude Code in every time.

You do not move any more files from here. Claude installs its own kit.

Step 1 Open Claude Code in the capcut-kit folder

Open Claude Code and open the capcut-kit folder you just placed inside your Claude folder.

Step 2 Paste this, exactly as written

Claude will take it from here: checking your Mac, installing a couple of free helper tools, and walking you through the rest one step at a time.

06 / The One-Time Style Setup

This is the one CapCut project you build by hand. It teaches the kit your CapCut's exact format and, for rough-cut, loads the fonts and sounds it uses. Do it once. Never delete it afterward.

Claude walks you through this checklist live and checks your work when you say "done": a new CapCut project with a short video dragged onto the timeline first, one text box, and — if you want the full rough-cut treatment — a few fonts, animations, sound effects, and a sticker. The project gets renamed exactly STYLE_SETUP and CapCut gets closed completely before Claude checks it.

Just want long-cut for now?

Tell Claude that during setup. Long-cut only needs the short version of this checklist — one clip, one text box. You can come back and add the rest for rough-cut any time later.

07 / Daily Use

Every Time Open Claude Code, run the command, drag your video in

Type /rough-cut or /long-cut, then drag your video file into the chat. Claude asks a question or two, shows you what it wants to do, and only builds after you say yes. Then it opens CapCut for you with the draft ready.

Two habits
  • Close CapCut before a build. Claude reminds you every time.
  • Keep a "B Roll" folder in Documents with short clips of yourself, named by what they show ("me pouring water.mp4"). Rough-cut uses it to suggest cutaways.

USER-GUIDE.md inside the kit is the full daily-use manual, including how to ask for changes ("rewrite the hook," "captions up a smidge").

08 / Make The Style Yours

The kit ships with one proven look. You don't have to keep it.

Find your own style inspiration, creators whose captions and text you love, and tell Claude what you want changed: fonts, colors, text positions, sounds. Claude updates the style for every video after that.

09 / If Something Breaks

  • A video that worked last week suddenly fails. CapCut updates itself sometimes, and an update can confuse the kit's format matching. Just tell Claude: "this worked before, CapCut updated." There's a repair guide built into the kit and Claude follows it. Your videos and your existing drafts are never at risk.
  • Claude says it doesn't know /rough-cut or /long-cut. Make sure setup finished and start a fresh Claude Code session — skills load when a session starts.
  • Still stuck. Bring it to the weekly call — two minutes of screen share solves what an hour of guessing won't.
The point of all this

Thirty minutes of setup buys you an editor that already knows your CapCut, your style, and your footage. Your job stays the human part: shoot your life, pick your videos, and make the final call on what ships.
