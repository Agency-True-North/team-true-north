New Members Pre-Mentorship Getting Started
Read this first

Mentorship starts on the 1st of each month. Use this guide to make the most of the time between now and then. You don't have to wait to get moving — the people who hit the ground running on day one started before day one.

The Three Phases

  1. 01 Day 1 — Get set up
  2. 02 First week — You
  3. 03 When your machine arrives

Phase 01 / Day 1 — Get Set Up

Do these today. They take less than 30 minutes and you'll thank yourself later.

Admin Essentials
  • Log in to your DSP (Distributor Support Portal) and confirm you can access your account.
  • Save your local Enagic Office as a contact in your phone. Go to enagic.com/en/contact, enter your location, and save the number.
  • Create a contact in your phone named ENAGIC. In the notes field, save all of your distributor IDs so they're always one tap away.
  • Block every mentorship call in your calendar right now. Treat them like non-negotiable appointments — because they are. Mentorship starts the 1st of next month. Don't let anything get scheduled over these.
  • Introduce yourself in the community group — your name, where you're from, and one word for how you're feeling right now.

Phase 02 / First Week — You

Before you learn how to talk to others, get clear on who you are, what you want, and why this matters to you. This is the work the masterclass can't do for you.

Your Why & Your Goal
  • Write your WHY. Not what you want to earn — what you're ultimately building toward, and for whom. Be honest. Be specific. This is for you, not for social media.
  • Set a written 90-day goal — a specific, honest number or outcome. Not a vague intention. Put it somewhere you will see it every single day.
  • Find your accountability buddy. Someone who will hold you to your word, inside or outside the community. Optional Highly recommended if this is your first time working for yourself.
Your Origin Story
Don't post these yet

These videos don't go live until mentorship starts. Save them. They become your most powerful launch content — and they're impossible to recreate once the moment has passed.

  • Record a raw, honest, to-camera video about why you decided to join. Don't script it. Don't perform it. Just speak the truth — what made you say yes, what you were feeling, what you're stepping away from or stepping toward. This is the most powerful piece of content you will ever create. It doesn't need to be perfect — it needs to be real.
  • Cast your vision on camera — where are you going? What does your life look like in 12 months if this works? Be bold. Be specific. Let people see the person you're becoming. This can be a second take of the same video, or a separate recording. Save it — don't post it yet.
Social Media Teasing
The goal of these posts

Your audience doesn't know anything is coming yet. These aren't about the product — they're about building curiosity before your official launch post drops when mentorship starts. No product name. No details. Just enough to make people curious.

  • Post your first tease. Something like: "I did a thing and I can't stop thinking about it. More soon." No product name, no details. Just enough to make people curious.
  • Respond to every comment and DM that comes from your tease. Nurture the curiosity — don't reveal too much, just keep the conversation warm.

Phase 03 / When Your Machine Arrives

Your machine typically arrives within the first one to two weeks. When it does, this is your moment — document everything.

Unboxing & Set-Up
  • Film or photograph the unboxing — the moment the box arrives, the packaging, the machine coming out. Your audience will feel the excitement with you.
  • Set up your machine following the setup guide. Take photos or short clips as you go — this becomes future content and helps you explain the process to customers later.
  • Use the machine for the very first time and capture your genuine reaction — a video, a voice note, a photo. Raw and real is the goal. This is content gold.
  • Go through the 68 uses doc and get familiar with everything your machine can do.
  • Start your daily hydrogen water selfie series. Content Idea Every morning when you drink your hydrogen water, grab a quick selfie video. Say "Day 1, Day 2," etc. By the end of the first month, you have a reel. Print your 30-Day Water Tracker and put it on your fridge — X off each day so you don't miss one.
Keep the Tease Going
  • Post a tease using your unboxing or set-up content — a close-up, an "it's here!" story, or a behind-the-scenes moment. Still no full reveal yet.
  • Write your business launch post this week — your story, what you've joined, why you chose it, and a clear invite for people to reach out. Write it from the heart, not from a template. Don't post it yet. Share it with your mentor or accountability buddy for feedback first.
