01 / How the Funnel Works

A lead enters your funnel when they sign up for the free masterclass. Everything that happens after that is handled by your email automations — automatically, without you doing anything.

Full funnel schematic — ad to long-term nurture

Here's the sequence in plain terms:

  1. Someone sees your ad and opts in for the free masterclass. They land on the Evergreen Webclass page — a video sales letter page where they can watch immediately. Meta does not allow countdown timers on ad landing pages, so the Evergreen page (no timer) is where they go first.
  2. They immediately receive the Welcome Email — a single email that confirms their registration and gives them the link to watch.
  3. The link in that email — and in every Push to Attend email that follows — goes to the Urgency Webclass page, which has the 3-day countdown timer. The timer is in the email funnel, not on the initial landing page.
  4. Over the next 3 days, they receive the Push to Attend + Cart Close series — 9 emails designed to get them to click through to the Urgency Webclass and watch before the timer expires.
  5. If they didn't buy by Day 3, the timer expires and the page redirects to a Closed Page. They enter the Building Trust / Re-Engagement series — 14 emails over 7 days. No selling. No masterclass link. Just trust-building.
  6. After the re-engagement series ends, the cart reopens. They receive the Cart Reopen series — 6 emails over 3 days linking back to the Evergreen Webclass (same video, no timer, cart is back open).
  7. Whether they buy or not at that point, they enter Long-Term Nurture — an ongoing email sequence that runs Monday, Wednesday, and Friday indefinitely, oscillating between value and push emails.
Two webclass pages — know the difference

The Evergreen Webclass is where leads land after opting in from the ad, and where the Cart Reopen series sends them. No countdown timer. Always available. The Urgency Webclass is what the Push to Attend emails link to — it has the 3-day countdown timer that cookies the browser. When the timer hits zero, the cart closes and the page redirects. The timer lives in the email funnel, not on the ad landing page.

02 / The Five Email Series

Six series. Each one has a specific job. Don't mix them. Don't combine them. Each one earns its place.

Series 1

Welcome Email

1 email  ·  Sends immediately at registration

The only email in the first workflow. Confirms their registration, delivers the masterclass link, and sets the tone. Subject line is tested and proven — don't change it.

Series 2

Push to Attend + Cart Close

9 emails  ·  Days 0–3 of the urgency window

Runs while the 3-day countdown timer is live. Gets increasingly urgent as the deadline approaches. Uses itchy bullets to create curiosity and drive replays. The final emails are cart-close emails — the cart is closing, not "going on sale."

Series 3

Push to Cart Close

3 emails  ·  Day 4  ·  Spaced through the day

A separate series from Push to Attend. By Day 4 the urgency window has closed. These 3 emails skip the webclass entirely and send leads directly to the checkout page. The tone shifts from "go watch" to "this is the last day to get in." Morning, afternoon, and a final send in the evening. After tonight, the cart is closed.

Series 4

Building Trust / Re-Engagement

14 emails  ·  7 days  ·  2 per day

Starts after the cart closes. No links. No masterclass mentions. This is purely trust-building — each email addresses one objection or fear and weaves in real story and testimonials. The goal is to keep the relationship warm so they're ready when the cart reopens.

Series 5

Cart Reopen

6 emails  ·  3 days  ·  2 per day

Reopens access to the masterclass via the Evergreen Webclass page. Honest urgency — the cart is open again, but not forever. Links in every email. Before building this sequence in GHL, turn the Advance Window OFF in the workflow settings. This controls whether GHL sends "missed" scheduled emails to contacts who enter the workflow after a send time has already passed that day — leaving it ON can cause contacts to receive multiple emails at once instead of spaced out correctly.

Series 6

Long-Term Nurture

Ongoing  ·  Mon / Wed / Fri at 9am

The indefinite sequence. Alternates between value emails (5) and push emails (5) and repeats. Value emails build trust and connection. Push emails include the masterclass link and a soft call to action. This is the long game.

04 / GHL: How to Upload Your Emails

Write all your emails in Google Docs first. Every email. All of them. Then upload to GHL. Do not write in GHL directly.

Google Docs gives you a real editing environment, spell check, easy sharing for feedback, and a clean record of your sequence. GHL's email editor is fine for formatting, but it's not a writing tool. Drafting there leads to errors, lost work, and sequences that are harder to review.

The process Google Docs first, GHL second
  • Write in Google Docs. One document per series. Label each email clearly: Email 1 — Subject Line, Email 2 — Subject Line, etc. Include the preview text for each email directly below the subject line.
  • Get feedback before uploading. Once a series is written, have someone read through the full sequence before you touch GHL. Easier to catch problems in Docs than after it's live.
  • Upload to GHL in order. Build each email in the correct automation workflow. Set the delay between emails according to the timing in that series' guide.
  • Test before activating. Send test emails to yourself. Check subject lines, preview text, links, and button text. Confirm your Urgency Webclass link and Evergreen Webclass link are correct.
  • Activate the workflow. Only after testing. Not before.
Important

Your Urgency Webclass link and your Evergreen Webclass link are different URLs. Do not mix them up. The Urgency page has the countdown timer. The Evergreen page does not. Using the wrong link in the wrong series breaks the experience for the lead.

06 / Deliverability Rules

These rules are non-negotiable. One violation can land your entire list in spam and tank your open rates permanently.

Always What your emails must do
  • Plain text format only. No custom fonts, no heavy HTML formatting, no elaborate design. Emails should look like they came from a real person, not a marketing department.
  • Compressed images only. If you include any image (most emails won't), it must be compressed before upload. Large images trigger spam filters.
  • One clear call to action per email. One link. One button. One thing you're asking them to do. More than one dilutes the action and looks like spam.
  • Write conversationally. First person. Real sentences. Not bullet-point marketing copy. The more it reads like a text from a friend, the better it performs.
Never What to keep out of your emails
  • No money language. Never write "financial freedom," "passive income," "make money," "earn from home," or any variation. These words trigger spam filters and instantly kill deliverability.
  • No income claims. Do not quote specific earnings, commissions, or results. Not yours, not Shannon's, not anyone's — not even general ranges.
  • No pressure tactics. Urgency is fine when it's real (the countdown timer is real). Manufactured urgency is not. Your leads can tell the difference.
  • No mention of the masterclass in the Re-Engagement series. That series is trust-only. Zero references to the offer, the product, or the opportunity.
