01 / What This Series Does

Series 3 closed the urgency window. This series opens the relationship. Your lead didn't buy yet — and that is not a failure. Most buyers come from here.

The people who go through this series watched your webclass, sat with it, and didn't take the next step. That doesn't mean no. It means not yet. Series 4 is how you stay visible and earn trust while they're deciding. Two emails a day for seven days. No selling. No pressure. Just consistent, honest, human contact.

The emails in this series do one of a few things: they share a piece of your real story, they name something your ICP is living through, or they offer a reframe. They never ask for anything. They just show up.

At a glance Series 4 overview
  • Number of emails: 14 (2 per day for 7 days)
  • Trigger: Day 5 — after Series 3 completes
  • Duration: Days 5–11 of the funnel
  • Links to: Nothing. No links in any email.
  • Goal: Stay in relationship. Build enough trust that when the window reopens in Series 5, they're ready to say yes.

03 / Example Emails

Two representative examples are shown below — one story email and one analogy email. They illustrate the tone, the length, and the format. The full swipe file in Section 06 has complete sequences from multiple affiliates.

These are examples, not templates

Do not copy these directly. Read them to understand what a good Building Trust email looks and feels like. Then go to the swipe file, read full sequences, and write your own. Every email in this series needs to come from your actual life and speak to your specific ICP.

Example — Story Email
Subject I almost didn't send this
Preview Not something I talk about much

Hey,

I've been thinking about you.

The woman who signed up to watch something that could potentially change things.

And I feel like I can say I know where you might be right now, because I was that woman.

She reads things twice. She thinks before she acts. She has people depending on her, and she takes that seriously.

And she's exhausted in a way she can't quite explain to anyone around her. A tired that lives in her bones.

I was her three years ago.

Not visibly falling apart. By most people's standards, I was actually doing pretty well. But I remember standing in my kitchen one night after everyone was asleep — the dishwasher humming, the counter cold under my arms, my son's toys on the floor.

And I sat there looking around at everything I had worked so hard to build, and thinking: how am I this exhausted, and we're still just getting by?

This quiet, hollow feeling. Like I had slowly disappeared into all the responsibilities that just kept falling on me.

I didn't tell anyone that. Because what was I supposed to say? That felt ungrateful. Crazy even.

So I just kept going.

It wasn't. I just hadn't been shown another way yet.

If any part of that felt familiar, I'm so glad you're still here.

With love,
Your Name

Example — Analogy / Reframe Email
Subject I always feel like the bionic woman on these
Preview then it hit me…

I was at the airport coming home from a work trip last week and found myself on one of those moving walkways down this long corridor.

You know the ones.

I always feel like the bionic woman on them. Just sailing past the people walking in the middle, completely unbothered. The Type A in me absolutely loves it.

And then it hit me. This walkway is exactly what systems did for my business.

For years I thought I just needed to work harder. Manage my time better. Be more productive. I was always one productivity hack away from the life I wanted.

But I could never get ahead. No matter how fast I walked I was still just walking.

Then I found a different way to build. And it was like stepping onto that moving walkway.

You could be the fastest walker in that airport. Bags rolling, coffee in hand, dodging everyone in your path. And the woman strolling calmly on the walkway will still get there first.

Not because she's better. Not because she has more. Because the ground underneath her is doing something yours isn't. It's working for her.

That's not a hustle problem. That's a vehicle problem.

And for the first time in my life, I stopped trying to walk faster and started paying attention to what I was walking on.

Everything changed.

With love,
Your Name

04 / Writing Guidance

These emails are story emails. They don't sell anything. They don't go anywhere. They just show up and tell a true thing in a way that makes your reader feel less alone.

Email types What to write and when

Most emails in this series fall into one of three categories:

  • Story email. A specific moment from your life — a night, a conversation, a realization. Short paragraphs, real detail, a clear emotional truth at the end. This is the most common type in the series.
  • Analogy or reframe email. A comparison that shifts how your reader sees the problem — the moving walkway, the pipeline vs. the bucket, the dog on a nail. Draws from something ordinary, lands somewhere surprising.
  • Objection email. You had the same fear or hesitation your reader has. You name it honestly, tell how you moved through it, and let the story do the work. Do not argue. Do not pitch. Just tell what's true.
How to write your version The process
  • Look at the timing table. Know what job each email is doing — what emotion it's naming or what objection it's addressing.
  • Find your story for that slot. What moment from your actual life maps to that job? It doesn't need to be dramatic. It needs to be specific and true.
  • Read examples from the swipe file before you write — not to copy, but to calibrate the tone and length.
  • Paste your story into Claude. Give it context on your ICP and ask for help shaping it into a clean email. Read it out loud. If it doesn't sound like you, send it back.
  • Check the two rules before saving: No links. No masterclass mentions.
The two rules. Again.

No links. Not one. Not even a "check this out" with no anchor text. Nothing that sends her anywhere.

No masterclass mentions. No Discovery Process. No webclass. No urgency. No "I know you haven't joined yet." None of it. These emails exist to build trust, not to nudge. If either rule is broken, the series works against you.

05 / GHL Setup

The workflow already exists. You are uploading your email content into the actions that are already set up — not building from scratch.

Write first, upload second

All 14 emails should be written, reviewed, and finalized in Google Docs before you touch GHL. These emails take time to write well. Do not draft inside GHL.

Step by step Uploading Series 4 in GHL
  • Go to Automations > Workflows in GHL.
  • Open: 2. Pipeline 1 & 2 (The True North Method)
  • Open: THE TRUE NORTH METHOD WEBCLASS - LEAD TO SIGN UP
  • Open: 4- Webclass - Building Trust
  • Find each email action in the workflow. There are 14 email actions — two per series day. Upload your emails in order, matching each email to the correct action and send time.
  • For each email action: paste in your subject line, add your preview text, paste in your body copy. There are no links to add in this series.
  • Send yourself a test for each email. Read it fresh — if anything sounds generic or off, fix it before moving on.
  • Save in two places for each action:
    • Click Save Action inside the email action itself.
    • Click Save in the top right corner of the workflow.
No links to add — that's intentional

You will not be adding any URLs in this series. If GHL prompts you to add a link or you see a placeholder for one in the action, leave it blank or remove it. These emails have no CTA. That is the point.
