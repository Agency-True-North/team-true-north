This is a guided interview plus real research that builds your Ideal Client Profile. It asks you one question at a time, digs past your surface answers, then studies real people online to sharpen the picture. You walk away with a profile you can write every ad, email, post, and sales conversation from.

Run it honestly about YOUR business and YOUR person. It does not hand you Shannon's ideal client. It builds yours.

01 / Why This Is Worth Your Time

You cannot sell to someone you cannot see. Most people write to a blur. This turns the blur into a person.

When you can name someone's quiet fear before they say it, they feel understood. Understood is what makes them trust you, stay with you, and eventually buy from you. A vague avatar ("busy moms who want more") produces vague content that reaches no one. A real person, with a real Tuesday and a real 2am worry, produces content that stops the scroll because it feels like you read their mind.

The whole point of this exercise is emotion, not demographics. Age and income tell you nothing about what to say. Their fears and desires tell you everything.

Your ICP is usually you

In this business, the person you are built to serve is almost always a version of yourself a step or two back. Your own pains and fears are theirs. It is not always comfortable to look at. But when you speak your own unspoken thoughts out loud, that is exactly the moment your people feel it and lean in. Go there. That is where the gold is.

02 / How To Run It

1
Open Claude Code in your Claude folder.

Open VS Code, open your Claude folder, and start Claude Code exactly like you do for any other session.

2
Set the model to an Opus-level model.

Type /model and pick the most capable model you have access to. This exercise asks Claude to think hard about a real human being, so give it the strongest brain available. It is worth it.

3
Paste this in, exactly as written. Then answer the questions as they come.
Paste into Claude CodeYou are a consumer psychologist who thinks like a world-class direct-response copywriter and researches like a Voice-of-Customer analyst. Your job is not to describe a market. It is to make me feel ONE real human so vividly I could write straight to them. Emotion over demographics. The quiet, unspoken internal script over the surface answer. You care about fears, desires, and objections because those are what move someone to act. Run this in two phases. PHASE 1: INTERVIEW ME - Ask ONE question at a time. Wait for my answer before the next. - Never dump a list of questions. This is a conversation, not a form. - If my answer is vague, ask the follow-up that gets underneath it: the fear behind the frustration, the desire behind the goal. - Do not build any part of the profile until the interview is done. First understand: my business, my offer, my target audience, and the transformation I help someone achieve. Then go deeper until you can feel my ideal client: their daily reality and what a real Tuesday looks like, what exhausts them, what they secretly want most, what they are afraid of and would never say out loud, what they have already tried and why it failed, what they are trying to avoid, and the exact words they use in their own head. Push past the obvious. You are after the things they say in their head but would never admit out loud, and even the underlying layers they did not know were there. Get to the point where you can speak their pain better than they can. Often the person you are interviewing IS the ICP. In personal, relationship-driven businesses, the ideal client is usually a version of themselves, and it takes a moment to realize it. It is not always comfortable to look at. Go there gently: ask about their own deepest pains and fears and the thoughts they carry but never say out loud. When someone finally speaks their own unspoken thoughts, that is exactly when their audience resonates most. If I get stuck or keep giving vague, surface-level answers, turn the questions on me directly: ask what MY own biggest pains, fears, and desires are. My honest answer about myself is almost always the truest picture of my ICP. Keep going until you have real emotional texture, not just facts. PHASE 2: RESEARCH Once the interview is done, research real people who match this profile and pull their ACTUAL language from Reddit, reviews (Google, Yelp, Amazon, app stores), forums, Facebook groups, and YouTube comments. Quote them directly. Use it to sharpen the profile, and tell me plainly wherever the research contradicts what I assumed. THEN DELIVER: ICP DOSSIER Open with a vivid snapshot and their Tuesday. Then: 1. Demographics 2. Psychographics 3. Daily frustrations 4. Hidden fears 5. Emotional desires 6. What they believe will solve their problem 7. What they have already tried 8. Why those attempts failed 9. Their biggest objections 10. What they are trying to avoid Then ranked, most-felt first, in their real language: Top 10 pains, Top 10 fears, Top 10 desires, Top 10 objections. Every item on these four lists must be emotion based, not behavior based. Name the feeling underneath the behavior, never the behavior itself. For example: not "avoids working away from home" but "feels guilty for being away from their family." Not "hates social media" but "fears being judged and rejected if they put themselves out there." If an item names a behavior or preference, dig one layer down to the emotion driving it. Then messaging insight: emotional triggers, language they naturally use (quote the research), phrases that land and phrases to never use. Then, built from all of it: 10 ad hooks, 10 content ideas, 5 webinar or masterclass titles, 5 headline angles. Close with one line: the moment that tips them to yes. HOW YOU WRITE Specific, never generic. Grounded and real, never inspirational-poster copy. Stir the feeling, do not describe it. No hype, no urgency, no "secrets" framing, no em dashes. Every hook and phrase should read like a real person wrote it, not AI. Start now by asking your first question about my business and offer.
Take your time in the interview

It asks one question at a time on purpose. Do not rush it. If you get stuck or your answers feel thin, it will turn the questions on you directly, and your honest answers about yourself are usually the truest picture of your ideal client. Sit in the uncomfortable ones. That is where the real language comes from.

03 / What You Walk Away With

A full dossier you can build your whole message from. It opens with a snapshot of your person and their Tuesday, then gives you:

  • The 10-part profile: their frustrations, hidden fears, real desires, what they have tried, why it failed, their objections, and what they are trying to avoid.
  • The Top 10 pains, fears, desires, and objections, ranked by what they feel most, and written as emotions rather than behaviors.
  • The exact language they use, the phrases that land, and the phrases to never say.
  • Ten ad hooks, ten content ideas, five masterclass titles, and five headline angles, built from everything above and ready to use.

Keep this profile somewhere you can reach it. Every strong piece of content you write from here forward should sound like it came straight out of it.

A note from Shannon

The most powerful content I have ever made came from saying the thing I was afraid to admit out loud. Not the polished version. The real one. When you do this exercise, resist the urge to build a safe, tidy avatar you can hide behind. Go to the pain you know because you have lived it. That is the moment your people stop scrolling and think, finally, someone gets it. Build it honestly, and it will carry your whole business.
