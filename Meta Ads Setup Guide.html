If you skip steps or rush the setup, your ads will either not work or burn money showing up in front of the wrong people. Do it right once and you never have to touch this again.

01 / What You Need Before You Start

Before you open a single tab, make sure all of this is ready. Starting without it means stopping halfway through, which is the fastest way to mess something up.

Required Have these ready before you begin
  • A personal Facebook account. Meta ties everything to a personal profile. You need this to get in. Your personal profile won't be visible on your ads — it's just identity verification.
  • Your GHL funnel, built and live. Each page needs a real URL before you can set up tracking. If your funnel isn't built yet, stop here, build it, then come back.
  • A credit or debit card. You'll add this as your payment method. Meta charges it when your ads run. You control spend through your daily budget settings.
  • A desktop or laptop computer. Not your phone. This is non-negotiable.
On funnel URLs

You need the exact URL for each page in your funnel: your opt-in page, your thank you page, your masterclass or video page, and your Discovery Process booking page. Open GHL and have those URLs written down or in a notes doc before you start.

02 / Create Your Meta Business Portfolio

Think of this as setting up the office building where everything lives. Your ads, your pixel, your payment — all of it goes inside this one account. You build the building first.

1

Go to business.facebook.com

Open a new browser tab and type business.facebook.com into the address bar. Hit enter.

2

Click "Create an account" or "Get Started"

You'll see a blue button at the top of the page. Click it.

3

Log in with your personal Facebook account

Meta will ask you to log in. Use your regular personal Facebook login. This is normal. Your personal profile won't be public-facing on your ads — it just verifies who you are.

4

Fill in your business details

You'll be asked for three things:

  • Business name. Use your actual business name, or your full name if you don't have one yet. Keep it professional.
  • Your name. Exactly as it appears on your ID.
  • Business email address. Use a professional email you actually check — not a Gmail you forgot the password to.
5

Click Submit, then verify your email

After you submit, Meta will send a confirmation email. Check your inbox, find the email from Meta, and click the verification link inside it.

Watch for this Check your spam folder if the email doesn't show up within a few minutes. It comes from a Facebook or Meta domain.

Your Meta Business Portfolio is now set up.

03 / Set Up Your Ad Account

Your Business Portfolio is the building. Your Ad Account is the room where the ads actually live. This is where your budget, campaigns, and payment info will go.

1

Open Business Settings

You should now be inside Meta Business Manager. Look for the left-hand menu and click Settings — or look for a gear icon. On some versions of the interface it's labeled Business Settings.

2

Find "Ad Accounts" in the left menu

Inside Settings, look at the left sidebar and click Ad Accounts.

3

Click "Add" then "Create a new ad account"

You'll see a blue Add button. Click it, then from the dropdown choose Create a new ad account.

4

Fill in your ad account details

  • Ad account name. Something you'll recognize — your name or business name followed by "Ads" works fine.
  • Time zone. Set it to your actual time zone. This affects how your ad performance data is reported. Wrong time zone = misleading data.
  • Currency. USD, or your local currency.
5

Click "Create ad account"

When it asks who this ad account is for, select My business.

You now have an ad account

Before you can run any ads, you'll need to add a payment method. That's the next step.

04 / Add Your Payment Method

1

Go to Payment Methods in Business Settings

Still inside Business Settings. Look in the left menu for Payment Methods and click it.

2

Click "Add payment method"

Select credit or debit card and enter your card information.

3

Click Save

That's it. Meta will charge this card when your ads run. You control how much you spend by setting a daily budget inside each campaign — Meta will never charge more than what you set.

05 / Create Your Meta Pixel

A pixel is a tiny invisible piece of code that lives on your funnel pages and reports back to Facebook: who visited, who signed up, who booked a call. It's how Facebook learns who your people are — so it can go find more of them.

Why this matters

No pixel means Facebook is flying blind. Your ads will cost more, reach the wrong people, and never get smarter over time. The pixel is what makes your ad strategy compound. Do not skip this.

1

Open Events Manager

From the main menu in Business Manager, look for Events Manager and click it. If you can't find it in the left sidebar, click the grid of dots icon at the top left — Events Manager will be in the list of tools that appears.

2

Click "Connect Data Sources"

You'll see a green button that says this. Click it.

3

Select "Web" then "Meta Pixel"

It will ask what kind of data source. Choose Web. On the next screen, choose Meta Pixel. Then click Connect.

4

Name your pixel

Use something clear — your business name or your name followed by "Pixel" is fine. You only need one pixel, so name it something you'll recognize a year from now.

5

Enter your website URL and continue

Type in your main funnel URL. This is just for Meta's reference — it doesn't lock you in to any specific pages.

6

Choose "Install code manually"

On the next screen it will ask how you want to install the pixel. Select Install code manually.

7

Copy the pixel code and save your Pixel ID

A block of code will appear. Click Copy Code to copy all of it. Before you leave this screen, also find your Pixel ID — a long number that looks like 123456789012345. Write it down. You'll need it.

Important Do not close this tab until you have both the code copied and the Pixel ID written down. Open a notes document and paste both there before moving on.

06 / Install the Pixel in Your GHL Funnel

There are two types of code you'll be working with. Understanding the difference is the most important thing in this entire guide.

Type 1 Base Pixel Code

This is the full block of code you just copied from Events Manager. It goes on every page of your funnel. You paste it once at the funnel level and it applies everywhere automatically. It tells Facebook "someone visited a page."

Type 2 Event Codes

These are short, one-line snippets that go on specific pages only. They tell Facebook exactly what happened on that page: "someone signed up," "someone booked a call." Each event code goes on the single page it belongs on.

Step A: Paste the Base Pixel Code at the Funnel Level

This applies the tracking to every page at once. You only do this once per funnel.

1

Open GHL and go to your funnel

Log in to GoHighLevel. In the left sidebar, click Sites, then click Funnels. Find your funnel and click on it.

2

Open the funnel Settings

Look at the top of the screen for a gear icon or a tab labeled Settings. Click it. This opens the settings for the entire funnel — not a single page.

3

Find the "Header Tracking Code" field

Scroll through the Settings panel until you see a section called Tracking Code or a text field labeled Header Tracking Code. Click into that box.

4

Paste your base pixel code

Paste the full code you copied from Meta. It will look like this (your Pixel ID number will already be filled in if you copied it directly from Meta):

Base Pixel Code — Paste this into funnel-level Header Tracking Code<!-- Meta Pixel Code --> <script> !function(f,b,e,v,n,t,s) {if(f.fbq)return;n=f.fbq=function(){n.callMethod? n.callMethod.apply(n,arguments):n.queue.push(arguments)}; if(!f._fbq)f._fbq=n;n.push=n;n.loaded=!0;n.version='2.0'; n.queue=[];t=b.createElement(e);t.async=!0; t.src=v;s=b.getElementsByTagName(e)[0]; s.parentNode.insertBefore(t,s)}(window, document,'script', 'https://connect.facebook.net/en_US/fbevents.js'); fbq('init', 'YOUR_PIXEL_ID_HERE'); fbq('track', 'PageView'); </script> <noscript><img height="1" width="1" style="display:none" src="https://www.facebook.com/tr?id=YOUR_PIXEL_ID_HERE&ev=PageView&noscript=1" /></noscript> <!-- End Meta Pixel Code -->
Check your Pixel ID

If you copied the code directly from Meta's Events Manager, your actual Pixel ID number is already in the code — you won't see "YOUR_PIXEL_ID_HERE." If you're pasting from notes, replace that placeholder with your real Pixel ID number before saving.

5

Click Save

The base code is now on every page of your funnel. Every visitor will be tracked.

Step B: Add Event Codes to Specific Pages

Now you'll add individual event codes to specific pages. These go in the page-level code settings, not the funnel-level settings you just used.

To get to a page's individual code settings in GHL:

  1. Inside your funnel, click Edit on the specific step or page you want to update. This opens the page builder.
  2. Look at the top left of the page builder. You'll see a small icon that looks like this: </>. It's labeled Tracking Code. Click it.
  3. A panel will open where you can paste your event code.
  4. Paste the event code for that page (from the table below).
  5. Click Save. Then go back to your funnel, click Edit on the next page, and repeat.

Which Code Goes on Which Page

Funnel Page What It Is Event Code to Paste
Thank You Page
After opt-in		 The page they land on right after submitting their email address <script>fbq('track', 'CompleteRegistration');</script>
Masterclass / Video Page Where they watch your content <script>fbq('track', 'ViewContent');</script>
Discovery Process Booking Page Where they schedule their call with you <script>fbq('track', 'InitiateCheckout');</script>
Booking Confirmation Page The confirmation page after they successfully book <script>fbq('track', 'Lead');</script>
Why Lead fires on the confirmation page, not the opt-in page

The Lead event is most valuable when it fires after someone actually completes the action — not when they just land on a page. Putting Lead on the opt-in page would fire it every time someone visits, even if they immediately leave without doing anything. On the confirmation page, it fires only when a real action happened. Facebook uses this signal to find people like your actual leads, not just your traffic.

If you have a purchase confirmation page, use this code:

Purchase Event — Only on purchase confirmation pages<script> fbq('track', 'Purchase', {value: 0.00, currency: 'USD'}); </script>

Replace 0.00 with the actual dollar amount of the purchase.

07 / Verify It's Working

Before you spend a single dollar on ads, confirm the pixel is actually firing. Five minutes of verification here saves hundreds of dollars later.

1

Open Chrome on your computer

This verification tool only works in the Chrome browser. If you don't have Chrome, download it first (it's free at google.com/chrome).

2

Install the Meta Pixel Helper extension

In Chrome, go to the Chrome Web Store and search for Meta Pixel Helper. It's a free browser extension made by Meta. Click Add to Chrome to install it. You'll see a small blue Facebook icon appear in your browser toolbar after installation.

3

Open your funnel's landing page

In the same Chrome window, open the first page of your funnel. Look at the Meta Pixel Helper icon in your toolbar.

What you want to see The icon should turn green and show a number (like "1" or "2"). If it's green, your pixel is firing. Click the icon to see exactly which events are detected on that page.
4

Click through every page in your funnel

Visit each page one by one. On each page, click the Pixel Helper icon and confirm the correct event is firing. Cross-reference with the table in Section 06.

If the icon stays gray or shows an error

The pixel code wasn't saved correctly. Go back into GHL, find the Header Tracking Code field in your funnel settings, confirm the code is there and complete, and click Save again. Then reload the page and check again.

08 / Connect Your Pixel to Your Ad Account

One last step. Your pixel needs to be officially connected to your ad account before you can use it when building campaigns.

1

Go to Business Settings > Data Sources > Pixels

In Meta Business Manager, open Business Settings. In the left menu, look for Data Sources, then click Pixels.

2

Click on your pixel

You'll see the pixel you created. Click on its name.

3

Click "Add Assets" and select your Ad Account

Click the Add Assets button. From the list that appears, select your Ad Account.

4

Click Save Changes

Your pixel is now connected to your ad account. When you build your first campaign, you'll select this pixel as your tracking source.

09 / Quick Reference Checklist

Go through this before you launch any ads. Every box needs to be checked.

  • Meta Business Portfolio created and email confirmed
  • Ad Account created inside Business Manager
  • Time zone and currency set correctly on Ad Account
  • Payment method added to Ad Account
  • Meta Pixel created in Events Manager
  • Pixel ID written down and saved somewhere safe
  • Base pixel code pasted into GHL funnel Header Tracking Code (all pages)
  • CompleteRegistration event code on Thank You page (after opt-in)
  • ViewContent event code on Masterclass / Video page
  • InitiateCheckout event code on Discovery Process booking page
  • Lead event code on booking Confirmation page
  • Pixel verified using Meta Pixel Helper Chrome extension on every page
  • Pixel connected to Ad Account in Business Settings > Data Sources

10 / Common Mistakes That Kill Results

Mistake 1 Skipping the pixel

Running ads without a pixel is advertising blindfolded. Facebook can't learn who to target. Your cost per lead will be high and your quality will be low. The pixel is what makes your ad spend compound over time — every conversion teaches Facebook who your buyers are. Do not run a single dollar without confirming the pixel fires correctly.

Mistake 2 Running ads from your personal Facebook profile

Always run ads through a Business Manager account, never through your personal profile. Personal profiles can be restricted without warning, can't be scaled, and don't have the full ad toolset available. If you set up Business Manager in Section 02, you're already doing this right.

Mistake 3 Wrong time zone on the ad account

If your time zone is set incorrectly, your performance data won't match your real business hours. You'll see your best-converting times as off-peak and your off-peak as peak. Make decisions based on bad data, get bad results. Double-check your time zone in Ad Account settings before you launch.

Mistake 4 Pasting the event code in the wrong place

The base pixel code goes at the funnel level — in funnel Settings, Header Tracking Code. Event codes go at the individual page level — in the page's own settings. Mixing these up either fires every event on every page (which corrupts your data) or fires nothing at all.

Mistake 5 Not verifying before launching

The Meta Pixel Helper takes five minutes. Not checking it before you turn on ads means you might run a full campaign with broken tracking and never know it. By the time you catch it, your ad data is worthless. Verify every time, before every launch.

If something looks different on your screen

Meta updates its interface regularly. If a button is in a slightly different spot than described here, don't panic. The names of things stay mostly consistent — look for the same words even if the layout has shifted. If you're stuck, reach out to your upline before clicking around and accidentally breaking something.

Now You Build the Campaign

Your infrastructure is live. The pixel is firing. Your ad account is connected to your funnel. That means when you're ready to launch, Facebook can immediately start learning who your people are.

The next step is building your first campaign inside Ads Manager — choosing your objective, defining your audience, uploading your creative, setting your budget, and going live. That's a separate guide.

What to have ready for that step:

  • Your ad creative (video or image, already filmed or designed)
  • Your ad copy (primary text, headline, and description)
  • Your daily budget (start with $15–$30/day while you're learning)
  • Your target audience details (location, age, interests)
