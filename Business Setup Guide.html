Welcome. You're building something real.

Before the ads run and the calls come in, you need a foundation under you. This is that foundation — laid out one step at a time.

Don't rush it. Don't do it all today. Check a box, breathe, come back tomorrow.

Order matters here. Each step sets up the next. Work top to bottom, and by the end you'll have a name, the handles to match, your registration, a domain, an email, a website, and your first ad live.

You've got a seat at this table. Here's how to set it.

01Name Your Business

Your name is the first thing people meet. It doesn't have to be clever. It has to be clear, easy to say, and easy to spell. Say it out loud. Text it to a friend. If they spell it back wrong, keep going.

Tip — Keep it short. Two or three words. Leave room to grow, so you're not boxed into one product down the road.
📷Photo to add: a business-name search in your state or country registry.

02Lock Your Handles

When someone sees your ad, the first thing they do is look you up. Your Instagram and Facebook need to match your name and each other. Same name. Same photo. Same energy. That match is what makes you believable.

Tip — If the exact name is taken, add a small, consistent tag — the same one everywhere. Never a different handle on each platform. Congruence is the whole point.
📷Photo to add: your matching Instagram and Facebook profiles, side by side.

03Register Your Business

This makes it real on paper. The exact steps depend on where you live — your state or your country sets the rules. Don't let that stall you. Most of it is one form and a small fee.

Tip — Keep business money and personal money apart from day one. It saves you at tax time.

Not sure which structure fits? A quick call with an accountant is worth the hour. Ask your sponsor who they use.

📷Photo to add: your state or country's business registration homepage.

04Claim Your Domain

Your domain is your address on the internet — yourname.com. Grab it now, even before the website exists. They're cheap, and the good ones go fast.

Tip — Reach for the .com first. It's the one people guess. Skip the strange endings.
📷Photo to add: a domain search showing your name available.

05Set Up Your Email

An address like you@yourbusiness.com beats yourname1987@gmail.com every time. It tells people you're serious before you say a word.

Tip — Keep it simple. One address is plenty on day one. You don't need ten.
📷Photo to add: your new branded email in the inbox.

06Build Your Website

Your site is where everything points. It doesn't need to be big. It needs to load fast, look like you, and tell people what you do and how to reach you.

Tip — One clear page beats five cluttered ones. Start small. You can grow it later.
📷Photo to add: your homepage on desktop and phone.

07Turn On Your Ads

Your foundation is set. Now you point traffic at it. You already have the full walkthrough for this part.

That's your foundation.

Name, handles, registration, domain, email, website, ads. You built the table.

Now you get to fill the seats. One step at a time — you don't have to have it all done today.

Come back, check the next box, keep moving.
