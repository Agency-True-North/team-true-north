Welcome. You're building something real.

Before the ads run and the calls come in, you need a foundation under you. This is that foundation — laid out one step at a time.

Don't rush it. Don't do it all today. Check a box, breathe, come back tomorrow.

Order matters here. Each step sets up the next. Work top to bottom, and by the end you'll have a clean business name, your real name on every platform, your registration, a domain, a professional email, and your first ad live.

You've got a seat at this table. Here's how to set it.

01Name Your Business

Keep it simple. Clear, concise, clean — not clever, not crazy. You want people to remember it and spell it right the first time.

Think of it this way: Jane Box is the person. Agency True North is the business. True North Funnels, the True North app — all clear, all clean. Your business name can be its own thing. It just has to be easy.

Tip — Clear beats clever every time. Short and clean carries further than unique and confusing.

02Use Your Real Name on Social

On social media, be exactly who you are. Your real name — first and last — on every platform. Not a clever handle. Not your business name. You.

People buy from a person they can see and trust. When your name and your face are the same everywhere, that's the congruence. It's what makes you believable when someone looks you up after the ad.

Tip — Same name, same face, everywhere. If your exact name is taken, add one small consistent piece — a middle initial, or your city — and use that same one on every platform.

Your business name and your social name don't have to match. Your business is its own thing. Your social is you.

03Register Your Business

This makes it real on paper. The exact steps depend on where you live — your state or your country sets the rules. Don't let that stall you. Most of it is one form and a small fee.

Tip — Keep business money and personal money apart from day one. It saves you at tax time.

Not sure which structure fits? A quick call with an accountant is worth the hour. Ask your sponsor who they use.

04Claim Your Domain

Your domain is your address on the internet — yourname.com. Pick it, buy it, and you're done. Our team takes it from there.

Tip — It has to be a .com. It's the one people guess and trust. If your exact name is taken, add your city — but keep it a .com.

05Set Up Your Email

Use Google Workspace. An address like yourname@yourbusiness.com beats a personal gmail every time. It tells people you're serious before you say a word.

Tip — Start with one address — your name. Down the road, as you grow, you might add a support@ address for your team. Plan for it, but you don't need it yet.

06Your Website — We Build It For You

Here's the good news: you don't build this. Once you have your domain, our team connects it to our Go High Level system and builds your funnel and site for you. All you do is get the domain and hand it off.

Tip — You focus on your name, your handles, and your first conversations. We'll handle the tech.

07Turn On Your Ads

Your foundation is set. Now you point traffic at it. You already have the full walkthrough for this part.

That's your foundation.

Name, real name on social, registration, domain, email, website, ads. You built the table.

Now you get to fill the seats. One step at a time — you don't have to have it all done today.
