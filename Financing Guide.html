01 / The Mindset Shift

Before the lenders and the links. This is the part that actually matters.

Most people will finance a $40,000 car without a second thought. You sign the papers, drive it off the lot, and it loses 20% of its value the same afternoon. You keep paying for years. Nobody calls that a risk.

Then someone considers financing $5,000 to $18,000 for a business, one where a single sale generates real income and can cover a month of payments, and suddenly it feels irresponsible.

That's not logic. That's conditioning.

What we finance without blinking What stops us cold
$40,000 car: loses 20% the day you own it. Pays you nothing. Ever. vs $5,000–18,000 business investment that can generate income from day one.
Average student loan: $37,000 with no guaranteed income attached, often paid for decades. vs Commission-based business: one sale can cover months of payments.
$20,000 kitchen renovation. Zero income potential. 100% consumption. vs Income-generating tool: each sale goes directly toward paying it off.
Two kinds of debt

There is debt that costs you money forever: cars, vacations, furniture. And there is debt that pays for itself: real estate, businesses, tools that generate income. The same credit card, the same loan, the same dollars. Completely different outcome depending on what you do with them.

What you're financing here is the second kind.

Why 0% credit cards are so powerful

Here's the part most people miss. A 0% introductory credit card means you are using the bank's money, for free, for 12 to 15 months. No interest. No cost to you.

You use the bank's money to purchase your machine. Your first customer buys from you. Their payment goes toward your card. Your second customer's commission covers another installment. You're building and running a business on other people's money the entire time.

That is not reckless. That is how business works. Every retail store, every franchise, every startup has used some version of this model. The question has never been whether it's okay to use financing. The question is what you're building with it.

The Math What this looks like in practice

With a 0% credit card, you are only required to make the minimum payment each month, typically 1 to 3% of your balance. On a $5,000 card, that's roughly $50 to $150 per month. You have 12 to 15 months to pay the full balance before any interest kicks in, and your commissions can go toward paying it down in whatever amounts work for you.

With a personal loan, payments are fixed. A $5,000 loan over 24 months is roughly $210 per month. A $10,000 loan over 24 months is roughly $420 per month.

A single Enagic commission ranges from $351 to over $7,000 depending on the machine sold.

One sale can cover months of minimum payments, or a significant chunk of your loan. The machine funds its own purchase. That is the business model working exactly as designed.

The right question to ask yourself

Wrong question: "Do I have all the cash right now?"

Right question: "Can I make this back?"

If the answer to the second question is yes, and it is, because the system works when you work it, then financing is not a risk. It's a starting line. Every month you wait to start is a month you're not building. The interest on a 12-month loan is often less than the income from one sale.

The resources below are organized by region. Start with what matches your location and your current credit situation. Apply strategically, not impulsively. Reach out to your coach before you submit anything — they can share what has worked for others in similar situations.

02 / United States

United States Financing

Four things to know before you apply

1. Apply to two cards right in a row. Most people need two cards to cover their full investment, since credit card companies don't tend to give high limits immediately. If you wait too long between applications, you may get denied, so apply to one right after the other. If you get denied by one, stop and regroup.

2. For good to excellent credit, Capital One Venture, Chase Freedom, Discover, and Citi are strong starting points. They offer 0% interest for 12 to 15 months and cash back on purchases. Genuinely useful.

3. Once approved, call and ask for a limit increase. Most companies will consider it if you ask. You can also request they expedite delivery of your card. This short video explains how to request a limit increase.

4. Talk with your coach before applying. They can share what has worked for others in similar situations.

Option 1 Credit Card Referrals

Best for: 670–850 credit score (good to excellent)

  • Wells Fargo: 0% intro APR
  • AMEX: on the spot approval; higher credit typically required
  • Capital One Venture One: 0% intro, travel rewards
  • Discover it: 0% intro APR, cash back
  • Citi Financial: 0% intro, 12 to 15 months
  • Bank of America: 12, 15, and 18-month 0% interest options available
Option 2 Loan Referrals & Banks

Best for: 630–719 credit score (fair to good)

Speaking with your own bank is often the best first step. They want to keep you as a customer. If you let lenders know the loan is for medical equipment (which is accurate), approval rates and limits tend to improve.

  • OneMain Financial: personal loans, flexible requirements
  • Upstart: credit check and personal loans
  • Fundwise: business funding options
  • Eaze Consulting: personal loans
  • Fiona: aggregator showing all potential options available to you (US only)
Option 3 Building Credit First

Best for: No credit / 300–669 credit score

If your credit isn't where it needs to be yet, this is the path. It's not a delay. It's the setup. Use a credit-building card to get to 650+, then apply for a larger loan.

  1. Apply for a starter card at Best Credit Cards for No Credit (2024).
  2. Put as many normal daily purchases on it as possible: food, gas, phone bill, utilities.
  3. Pay the entire balance off every two weeks without fail. This signals consistent on-time payments to lenders.
  4. Check your score monthly through Credit Karma.
  5. Once you reach 650 or above, you're positioned to apply for a larger loan.

03 / Canada

Canada Financing

Canadian Distributors: Action Required

Please send your coach a photo of your driver's license. This is required by the government to verify your identity when starting this business and must be submitted along with your registration forms.

Option 1 0–1% Interest Credit Cards

MBNA and BMO are the two lowest-interest options available in Canada. Both work as balance transfers: you pay for your product with another card first, then transfer the balance to lock in the low rate for 9 to 12 months. They can be used together or combined with another loan.

  • MBNA Mastercard: 0% interest (balance transfer)
  • BMO Mastercard: 0.99% interest (balance transfer)
Option 2 Personal Loans

A loan is one of the most common ways any business gets started. There will be interest, but the goal is to pay it off faster as your business generates income.

  • GoPeer: low interest for good credit; requires 3+ years of credit history
  • Tangerine: line of credit
  • Borrowell: quick loans up to $20,000
  • Fairstone: Canada's leading non-bank lender, $500 to $35,000
Option 3 Your Bank

If you have a solid relationship with your bank, go directly to them. They may be willing to increase your existing credit limit, open a new line of credit, or approve a personal loan, especially for an existing customer in good standing.

Option 4 Medicard

Medicard provides financing specifically for medical equipment and works with Enagic regularly. Many team members have used them successfully to finance a quad or trifecta.

A few important things to know:

  • Interest rates can be high. After approval, call or email and ask for a lower rate. They will often reduce it significantly. All you have to do is ask.
  • Most loans are structured for 5 to 6 years. Pay it off faster to minimize interest.
  • They ask for employment information to verify income only. They are not legally permitted to contact your employer about your business.

05 / Europe

Europe Financing

Before You Apply: Read This

Apply to two cards at the same time. Open two browser tabs and click the final submit button one right after the other. Applying simultaneously prevents lenders from seeing each other's applications in their initial review.

VAT note: There is currently no VAT charged at the point of purchase. It will be added separately and collected when your product is delivered. VAT is approximately 20% and will be paid at the door upon delivery.

Check your credit score first: experian.co.uk

Option 1 Money Saving Expert

0% interest for 12 to 24 months

Go to moneysavingexpert.com and search "0% money transfer." Most people with a job and a few years of UK residency are eligible. Look for cards with no interest for 12, 18, 24, or 36 months. Note: there is typically a small one-time fee.

Top picks from this tool:

  • MBNA
  • Tesco Bank
  • Virgin Money
Option 2 Money Supermarket

Moneysupermarket.com offers comparison tools across personal loans, credit cards (including 0% interest, cashback, credit-building, and bad credit options), and more. A solid place to explore what you qualify for.
