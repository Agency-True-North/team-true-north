01 / What The Engine Is

Most AI content fails for one of two reasons. It sounds like a robot wearing your name, or it sounds like you but sells nothing. The engine exists to kill both failures at once.

Here is the whole system in one flow. You speak a raw voice memo. The engine drafts from it, keeping your exact phrases wherever they are strong. A critic then scores the draft on two axes: does it sound like you, and does it pull the reader toward action. A revise pass fixes only what the critic flagged, your words stay untouched everywhere else, and the critic runs again. You only see drafts that passed both bars.

Why it will sound like you

During setup, the engine studies your real writing: emails you actually sent, posts, scripts, anything true to your voice. It extracts how you open, the sentence patterns you repeat, the words you would never use, and builds those into a spec it loads every single day. It never guesses at your voice. It learned it.

02 / Before You Start

1
Finish the Claude Setup Guide first.

The engine runs inside Claude Code in VS Code, and it reads the foundation files you built there. If you have not done that guide, do it before this one.

2
Gather your real writing.

Put your best real content into a folder inside your Claude folder: emails you actually sent, captions, scripts, anything that sounds like you at your best. Ten pieces is plenty. Five is workable. The engine refuses to build a voice from nothing, because a voice built from nothing is a generic voice.

If you have almost no written content yet

That is okay. Tell the engine during setup. It will lean harder on the interview and your foundation files, mark your voice spec as provisional, and sharpen it as you produce real content. Honest and thin beats fake and full.

05 / The Setup Interview

This is the one expensive step, and you only do it once. Give it a real session, not a spare ten minutes.

Claude will ask you, one question at a time: what you sell and what your content drives toward, who you serve, where your real writing lives, your hard rules, and what makes your audience tune out. Then it studies your writing and drafts your personal Voice Spec: your opener styles, your repeated sentence moves, your humor if you have it, your banned words, your sign-off.

You are the gate

The engine stops and shows you the Voice Spec before building anything else. Read it. If a section feels generic, say so, and it must sharpen that section from your real writing before moving on. Do not wave a generic spec through. Everything downstream inherits it.

Setup ends with a dress rehearsal: you speak one raw voice memo, and the full pipeline runs while you watch. The standard for passing is simple. It sounds like you, your exact phrases survived into the draft, and reading it makes you want to hit send.

Use the strongest model for setup

Type /model in Claude Code and pick the most capable model you have access to for this session. Setup quality is permanent. Daily runs can use a cheaper model precisely because setup was done well once.

06 / Daily Use

1
Speak a voice memo.

Record yourself talking through the idea, messy is fine, then get the transcript. Your phone's voice memo transcription works.

2
Run the engine.
/engine This is for a nurture email, no pitch on this one. [paste your voice memo transcript]

Tell it the format (email, ad, reel, caption) and it handles the rest: draft, critic, revise, critic again, and shows you the finished piece with a verdict.

3
Use the two switches when you need them.

Say "no pitch on this one" in your memo and the engine writes pure value, and will actually flag itself if a pitch sneaks in. Say "this one isn't funny" or "let it land heavy" and it keeps humor out entirely.

You can also run the critic alone on anything you wrote yourself: paste the draft and say "run the critic on this." It will tell you exactly which lines are weak and why, without rewriting you.

07 / Teaching It What Works

The engine ships smart and gets smarter, but only if you feed it results.

After a send or while an ad runs, tell Claude what happened in one line: "subject: [your subject line] worked" or "this hook is winning." It logs the result, and every future batch weights toward what has actually performed for YOUR audience. Real results outrank every rule in the engine. Ten seconds per result is the whole habit.

08 / Troubleshooting

1
Claude says it doesn't know /engine.

Paste this instead: "Follow engine/skills instructions: read content-engine-starter-main/skills/engine/SKILL.md and run it on this memo." Restarting Claude Code also registers the skills.

2
A draft doesn't sound like you.

Quote the exact line that feels off and say "this line isn't me, fix the voice spec, not just this draft." That upgrades every future draft instead of this one.

3
The critic keeps failing your drafts.

That usually means it is doing its job. Read the quoted lines in its report. But if it flags language you use on purpose, tell it: "that phrasing is sanctioned, add it to my approved language." The critic never flags your approved language twice.
