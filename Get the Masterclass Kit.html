What Happens Next

From that one prompt, Claude takes over — you just answer:

  • It starts from what it already knows. It reads your files and anything it can reach, then shows you what it already pieced together about your voice, audience, and offer — so you're not re-explaining a business it can already see.
  • It only asks about the gaps. One question at a time, on what's missing or unclear — not a from-scratch questionnaire. (If it can't find any of your writing anywhere, it'll ask you for a sample or two — it won't invent a voice.)
  • It shows you every file before saving it, and waits for your yes. You are the source; the final call is always yours.
  • It ends with proof. A few lines written in your voice, to your person, and one question: does this sound like you?

When setup is done, run /build-masterclass to write the full class. Your ideal-client profile is already built, because setup runs the full ICP interview inside it. Run /build-icp again only when you target a new audience. Everything reads your setup files automatically; you never explain your voice twice.

Why setup, and not just my files

Your voice and your ideal client are not mine. If you inherited someone else's calibrations, every class you made would sound like them. That's the whole reason setup exists. It builds your layer in one honest conversation, so the system sounds like you from the first line.
