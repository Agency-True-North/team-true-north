Action 3 Drag

Drag that whole folder into your Claude folder — the one you made in the Claude Setup Guide, next to ABOUT ME and PROJECTS. That's the last manual thing you'll do.

02 / Install — One Paste

Action 4 Open Claude Code in your Claude folder and paste this

Claude moves the files itself, confirms what it did, and starts your setup. If it asks permission to copy files, approve it.

Copy and paste this into Claude Code
Read content-engine-starter-main/README.md. Install the engine: copy the engine folder to my workspace root and the two skill folders into .claude/skills/. Then walk me through setup by following content-engine-starter-main/skills/engine-setup/SKILL.md step by step.
Use your strongest model for setup

Type /model and pick the most capable one you have. Setup happens once and everything inherits it — this is the wrong place to save money.

03 / Section 1 — The Interview

Claude asks, you answer — one question at a time. What you sell, who you serve, where your real writing lives, your hard rules, what makes your audience tune out.

  • Answer in your own words. Talk like you'd talk to a friend — that's the data it needs.
  • Point it at real writing: emails you actually sent, captions, scripts. Ten pieces is plenty, five works.
  • Thin on writing? Say so. It builds a provisional voice from the interview and sharpens as you go.
If setup didn't start on its own, paste this
/engine-setup

04 / Section 2 — Approve Your Voice Spec

Claude studies your writing and shows you a Voice Spec — how you open, your sentence moves, your banned words, your sign-off. It stops and waits. You are the gate.

  • Read every section. It should feel like looking in a mirror.
  • Anything generic — say so with the box below. Don't wave it through; everything downstream inherits this.
  • When it reads like you, say "approved."
If a section feels generic, paste this
This section reads generic, not like me. Sharpen it from my real writing before moving on — quote the lines of mine you built it from.

05 / Section 3 — The Critic Test

Claude calibrates its built-in critic, then proves it: it runs the critic on one of your older real pieces and shows you the report.

  • Read what the critic flagged. It should catch real weak spots, not style you chose on purpose.
  • If it flags language you use deliberately, sanction it with the box below.
If the critic flags something you say on purpose, paste this
That phrasing is sanctioned — it's deliberate. Add it to my approved language so the critic never flags it again.

06 / Section 4 — The Dress Rehearsal

The last setup step. You speak one raw voice memo, and the full pipeline runs while you watch — draft, critic, revise, critic again.

  • Record a real memo on your phone, messy is fine. Copy the transcript.
  • The pass bar: it sounds like you, your exact phrases survived, and reading it makes you want to hit send.
  • If it misses, quote the exact line that's off — Claude fixes the spec, not just the draft.
Paste this with your transcript
Here's my dress rehearsal memo. Run the full pipeline and show me the finished piece: [paste your voice memo transcript]
· · ·
Setup is done — everything below is daily life

You never repeat sections 1–4. From here on, the engine is a two-minute habit: speak, paste, read, send.

07 / Section 5 — Daily Runs

Speak a voice memo. Paste the transcript. Tell it the format. That's a run.

Copy, fill in, paste into Claude Code
/engine This is for a [email / ad script / reel / caption]. [paste your voice memo transcript]

Two switches you control inside the memo itself:

  • Say "no pitch on this one" — it writes pure value and flags itself if a pitch sneaks in.
  • Say "this one isn't funny" or "let it land heavy" — it keeps humor out entirely.

08 / Section 6 — Critic-Only Mode

Wrote something yourself? The critic will tell you which lines are weak and why — without rewriting you.

Copy, fill in, paste into Claude Code
Run the critic on this. Tell me which lines are weak and why — don't rewrite it: [paste your draft]

09 / Section 7 — Teach It What Works

Ten seconds per result. Real results outrank every rule in the engine.

After a send or while an ad runs, paste one line like these
subject: [your subject line] worked — open rate was strong this hook is winning: [the hook] this one flopped: [what it was] — log it

10 / If You Get Stuck

Claude says it doesn't know /engine — paste this

A draft doesn't sound like you — paste this
This line isn't me: "[paste the line]". Fix the voice spec, not just this draft.

Still stuck? Restart Claude Code first — that registers the skills. Then bring it to the weekly call; two minutes of screen share beats an hour of guessing.

The voice is yours

Don't copy anyone else's calibrations, voice rules, or ideal-client language — not Jane's, not Shannon's. The system is shared. The voice it learns is yours, from your writing and your people. That's the entire point of the interview.
